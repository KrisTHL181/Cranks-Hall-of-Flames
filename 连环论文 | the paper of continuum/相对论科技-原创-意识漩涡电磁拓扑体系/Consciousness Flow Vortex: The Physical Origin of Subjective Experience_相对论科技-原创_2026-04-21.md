## **2 Research Dilemma of Subjective Experience: Innate Defects of Information Logical Recursion**

Academic research on "recursive closed loops" has long remained at the level of information logical recursion, which has inherent limitations and cannot generate subjective experience. Its core defects are reflected in four dimensions:

### **2.1 Abstract Symbolic Circulation Without Physical Substance**

Information logical recursion occurs only at the abstract cognitive level, resembling a "conceptual game" where "sentences loop around sentences, thoughts refer to thoughts, and symbols point to symbols." It lacks real physical entities, energy flow, or spatial fields, essentially serving as a "description" rather than "existence" of the objective world, and thus cannot generate the real experience of "I am perceiving."

### **2.2 Discrete Circuits Without a Focused Center**

Logical recursion only has loops without a clear focused center; all information processing involves "step-by-step discrete jumps," failing to form a unified perceptual subject. In contrast, the core of subjective experience is the "first-person 'I'"—a unique, focused perceptual center. Discrete logical loops can never merge into a unified subject.

### **2.3 Fragmented Processing Without Integration**

Logical recursion involves fragmented information processing, where information from different senses and dimensions remains isolated. However, subjective experience is a continuous, unified whole (e.g., when seeing an apple, perceptions of color, shape, and taste merge into a single experience). Fragmented information processing cannot achieve such perceptual integration.

### **2.4 Static Processes Without Dynamic Characteristics**

Logical recursion lacks energy, flow, intensity, contraction, or diffusion, functioning as static symbolic computation. In contrast, subjective experience is a dynamic, continuous process (e.g., emotional fluctuations, focused or scattered attention). Static logical loops cannot match the dynamic nature of subjective experience.

In short, information logical recursion is merely "armchair speculation"—no matter how complex, it only involves the repetition and reference of information. Subjective experience, however, is a real physical existence. The two belong to different dimensions, which is the core reason why the academic community has been stuck for decades without breakthroughs.
