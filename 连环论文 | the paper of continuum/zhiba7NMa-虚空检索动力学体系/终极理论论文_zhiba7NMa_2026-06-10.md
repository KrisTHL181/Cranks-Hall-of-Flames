1. Introduction

The quest for a final theory of physics has traditionally aimed at a single mathematical structure that encompasses all known interactions. String/M-theory remains the leading candidate, yet it suffers from the landscape problem (vast number of vacua) and lacks testable predictions at accessible energies. More fundamentally, all physical theories share hidden assumptions: the equality sign, fixed constants, and the prohibition of divergences.

Here we propose a radical shift: instead of searching for a unique equation, we construct a meta-theory that controls how equations relate to observations. The Control Parameter Paradigm (CPP) replaces the notion of absolute equality with a controllable association A \;\infty_{\kappa,s}\; B, where \kappa is a set of control parameters (all physical constants and even logical rules) and s is a control strength. Divergences, which in standard quantum field theory require renormalization, are not eliminated but used to invert for \kappa. This turns “problems” into information.

We then show that by introducing a dynamical scalar field \varphi (the control field) that determines all \kappa via its vacuum expectation value, the CPP becomes a fully predictive quantum field theory. The low-energy limit reproduces the Standard Model and general relativity, while at high energies it incorporates M-theory as a specific equivalence class. The landscape of string vacua collapses to a unique solution once observational data are used to invert for the moduli parameters.
