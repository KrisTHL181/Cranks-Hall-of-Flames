题目：彻底解决 第三次数学危机 是一个幻想吗？

我们都知道，集合论中的悖论引发了第三次数学危机。一个多世纪以来，数学家们一直在致力于解决这个问题，目前主流的解决方案包括 策梅洛 - 弗兰克尔集合论 （ZFC）、 冯·诺伊曼 - 伯奈斯 - 哥德尔集合论 （NGB）以及 类型论 。

从一个观察者的角度，对同样的问题存在多种不兼容的解决方案，意味着这个问题并没有得到解决。而且从美学的角度去看，这些方案都臃肿复杂，似乎并不具备成为数学基础的资质。

有一个事实已经非常明显，造成这次危机的悖论， 最大序数悖论 ， 最大基数悖论 ， 罗素悖论 ，有一个共同的特点，就是自指。  自指如同一个并未扩散的肿瘤，只要用手术刀割掉似乎就解决了问题。 比如，我们可以在 康托尔集合论 的基础上，另外设立一条公理：任何集合都不包含自身，这其实是 正则公理 的一个功能。

但是， 哥德尔不完备定理 使得情况更加恶化。 因为90年来，数学界已经确认，哥德尔用严谨的数学方法构造出了一个自指命题，使得自指具有了合法性，因此强行割掉自指的做法显得不合情理。 也就是说，正则公理和哥德尔不完备定理有内在的冲突。

当数学家们为数学基础的危机而忧心忡忡的时候，物理学的发展却欣欣向荣。在罗素悖论之后， 相对论 ， 量子力学 井喷式地发展，并没有受到数学危机的任何影响。 因为数学是物理的语言，如果数学发生了危机，而物理却安然无恙，这本身就是一个无法解释的现象。并且我们注意到，自指在物理学领域从来都没有出现过。 如果物理天然禁止自指，那么在数学领域割掉自指就具有了合法性。但是如何解决与哥德尔定理的冲突呢？

综合考虑视野当中的所有问题，如果哥德尔定理是错的，那么整个世界就和谐了。 那么，是否有那么哪怕一点点的可能性，哥德尔定理是错误的呢？

Title: Is it a fantasy to solve the 3rd mathematical crisis completely?

Dear FOMers,

We all know that paradoxes in set theory triggered the 3rd mathematical crisis. Mathematicians have been working on this for more than a century with ZFC, NGB and type theory as the current mainstream solutions.

From the perspective of an observer, the existence of multiple incompatible solutions to the same problem means that the problem has not been solved. Moreover, from an aesthetic point of view, these solutions are all cumbersome and complex and do not seem to have the qualifications to be the foundation of mathematics. It has become very obvious that the paradoxes that caused this crisis - the maximum ordinal paradox, the maximum cardinal paradox, and Russell's paradox - have a common characteristic, which is self-reference.

Self-reference is like an unspread tumor, and it seems that the problem can be solved by cutting it out with a scalpel. For example, based on Cantor's set theory, we can establish another axiom: no set contains itself, which is actually a function of the axiom of regularity. However, Gödel's incompleteness theorem has made the situation worse. For 90 years, the mathematical community has confirmed that Gödel used rigorous mathematical methods to construct a self-referential proposition, making self-reference legitimate. Therefore, the practice of forcibly cutting out self-reference seems unreasonable. That is to say, there is an internal conflict between the axiom of regularity and Gödel's incompleteness theorem.

When mathematicians are worried about the crisis in the foundation of mathematics, the development of physics is thriving. After Russell's paradox, relativity and quantum mechanics have developed explosively and have not been affected by the mathematical crisis at all. Because mathematics is the language of physics, if there is a crisis in mathematics but physics remains unscathed, this in itself is an unexplainable phenomenon. And we note that self-reference has never appeared in the field of physics. If physics naturally prohibits self-reference, then cutting out self-reference in the field of mathematics is legitimate. But how to solve the conflict with Gödel's theorem?

Considering all the problems in the horizon, if Gödel's theorem is wrong, then the whole world will be harmonious. So, is there even a teeny-tiny possibility that Gödel's theorem, hailed as the highest fruit of human intelligence, is wrong?
