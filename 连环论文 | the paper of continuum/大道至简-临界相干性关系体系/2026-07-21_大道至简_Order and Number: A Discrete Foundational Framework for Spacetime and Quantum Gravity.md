# Order and Number: A Discrete Foundational Framework for Spacetime and Quantum Gravity

**Abstract.** This paper proposes a foundational framework for quantum gravity based on two primitive, mutually irreducible concepts: *order* (causal generation sequence) and *number* (cardinality of events). We argue that the continuum spacetime of general relativity—with its metric structure, causal relations, and volumetric measures—emerges as a statistical coarse-graining of a fundamentally discrete combinatorial substratum. In this framework, time corresponds to the sequential growth of a causal partial order, while space corresponds to the cardinality of events at a given causal slice. These two aspects are shown to be mathematically non-interchangeable, providing a natural resolution to the problem of time in quantum gravity, the origin of thermodynamic arrows, and the ultraviolet divergences of quantum field theory. We demonstrate compatibility with established results including Lorentz invariance in the continuum limit, the Page-Wootters mechanism for emergent time, and the sequential growth dynamics of causal set theory. Testable phenomenological consequences, including Lorentz invariance violation at Planck scales and the running of spectral dimension, are discussed.

**Keywords:** quantum gravity · causal set theory · discrete spacetime · problem of time · order and cardinality · emergent spacetime

## 1 Introduction

The search for a quantum theory of gravity remains one of the deepest open problems in theoretical physics. At the heart of this challenge lies a fundamental tension: general relativity conceives of spacetime as a continuous, dynamical four-dimensional manifold, while quantum mechanics presupposes an external, continuous time parameter for unitary evolution. The Wheeler-DeWitt equation, <!–MATH_PH_2–>, famously contains no time parameter, giving rise to the “problem of time” in quantum cosmology.

Various approaches have been proposed to resolve this impasse. Causal set theory (CST) postulates that at the most fundamental level, spacetime is discrete, with the continuum replaced by locally finite partially ordered sets or “causal sets”. In the continuum approximation of CST, the order relation corresponds to causality while cardinality (number of elements) corresponds to spacetime volume. The theory combines the “essence” of Lorentzian geometry with ultraviolet finiteness.

Meanwhile, the Page-Wootters formalism offers a relational mechanism for the emergence of time from entanglement between a system and a clock subsystem, treating the total system as a stationary (timeless) entangled state. In this framework, time arises as a relational observable rather than a fundamental parameter.

This paper synthesizes these insights into a unified foundational framework centered on two primitive, mutually irreducible concepts: **order** (causal generation sequence) and **number** (cardinality of events). We argue that these two concepts—and only these—are required as the primitive building blocks of a fundamental theory of spacetime, and that their non-interchangeability provides the deep reason for the asymmetry between time and space observed in the macroscopic world.

### 7.2 Running of the Spectral Dimension

In the order-number framework, the effective dimension of spacetime is expected to run with the energy scale. At low energies (large causal sets, many elements), the dimension is 4. At high energies (small causal sets, few elements), the dimension decreases.

This is consistent with results from asymptotic safety and causal dynamical triangulation, which find a reduction of the spectral dimension to 2 at Planck scales. The mechanism in the order-number framework is clear: at small scales, the number of accessible elements (cardinality) is small, and the causal order is sparse, so the effective geometry has lower dimensionality.

### 7.3 Cosmological Constant Fluctuations

Causal set theory has predicted fluctuations in the value of the cosmological constant that are consistent with observations. In the order-number framework, these fluctuations arise from the stochastic nature of sequential growth: the density of elements (cardinality per unit volume) fluctuates, and these fluctuations manifest as a fluctuating effective cosmological constant.

### 7.4 Resolution of the Big Bang Singularity

In the order-number framework, the big bang is not a singularity of infinite curvature but the initial stage of sequential growth—the birth of the first elements of the causal set. As one approaches the big bang, the number of elements decreases to a finite minimum, and curvature remains finite. This provides a natural resolution of the classical singularity without additional assumptions.

## 8 Conclusion

We have presented a foundational framework for quantum gravity based on two primitive, mutually irreducible concepts: **order** (causal generation sequence) and **number** (cardinality of events). The key results are:

1. **Order and number are mathematically non-interchangeable.** This provides the deep reason for the asymmetry between time and space in the macroscopic world and rules out any attempt to reduce one to the other.

2. **Time is sequential growth.** The temporal direction is not an emergent phenomenon but the primitive sequential structure of the causal set itself.

3. **Space is cardinality.** Spatial volume, and by extension all geometric structure, emerges from the counting of elements and the statistical properties of the causal order.

4. **Continuum spacetime emerges via faithful embedding.** The causal structure and metric of general relativity are recovered in the continuum limit.

5. **The problem of time is resolved.** Time is not a missing parameter but the intrinsic sequential growth of the causal set, with the Page-Wootters mechanism providing the relational description.

6. **Testable predictions** include Lorentz invariance violation at Planck scales, running of the spectral dimension, cosmological constant fluctuations, and resolution of the big bang singularity.

The order-number framework does not replace causal set theory but provides a deeper conceptual foundation for it, emphasizing the primacy and non-interchangeability of its two core elements. It offers a unified perspective on the emergence of spacetime, the arrow of time, and the quantum gravity problem—all from two simple primitives: **order** and **number**.
