A true story, the significance of which goes far beyond the story itself

-------------------------

My Dog Huzi: Deriving Great Wisdom = Half Wisdom

-------------------------

The core and most vivid anchor of all my theories on consciousness and intelligence does not come from arcane papers or sophisticated experiments, but from Huzi, a rural dog I have raised since he was born. It can be said that most of the underlying logic of the OFIRM framework was gradually deduced during daily walks with Huzi and observations of his interactions with other dogs.

I delivered Huzi myself and held him in my arms as a puppy; now he has grown so strong that I can no longer carry him. He is not particularly smart, struggles to learn many tricks, and his intellectual level is at most 0.5. His mind is driven only by the most primitive survival consciousness, without complicated self-scheming or human obsessions and entanglements. Yet his behavior perfectly embodies the realm that Buddha attained through a lifetime of practice. It was from him that I derived a subversive conclusion: Great Wisdom is equivalent to Half Wisdom.

I keep three dogs. I used to feed them in separate bowls, thinking it would keep the peace, but it was useless. Huzi is naturally dominant: after finishing his own food, he immediately steals from the other two. When they compete fiercely, they even bite each other until their legs bleed, entering a state of full stress with their fur bristling with aggression. Later, I changed my strategy and scattered the food across the floor, letting them eat together. At least each dog can fill up at the start, with competition only emerging over the last few kibbles.

Yet the most magical moment always comes at the end: once the last piece of food is gone and Huzi and the others confirm there is nothing left, the fierce fighting that drew blood just seconds before stops instantly. Stress dissipates immediately, their fur relaxes, and the three dogs act as if nothing happened. They lick each other and snuggle affectionately, holding no grudges, no resentment, no knots in their hearts.

This extreme state of letting go once things pass first connected Huzi to Buddha in my mind.

Buddha cultivated for a lifetime to let go of obsessions, abide without attachment, and remain unperturbed. When joy arises, he feels joy; when conflict comes, he stays calm. After things pass, his heart is free of burdens, unchained by emotions or fixations. Huzi is born this way. With only 0.5 intellect, free from tangled complex thoughts and human greed, anger, ignorance, arrogance, and doubt, he lives the life of Buddha.

When food and resources are present, his survival consciousness activates, his vibration peaks, and he fights, competes, and asserts dominance — a natural stress response driven by survival instinct.When food and resources are gone, his survival vibration resets to zero, obsessions vanish completely, and he returns to peace and kindness without resentment, internal struggle, or lingering negativity.

The "great wisdom" that humanity pursues throughout life is nothing but actively stripping away obsessions and returning to one’s true nature. With his innate 0.5 half-wisdom, Huzi already possesses this quality. He lacks a complex sense of self; all his actions revolve around survival consciousness, without redundant thoughts or unnecessary obsessions. This precisely proves the essence of consciousness: the level of intelligence never equals the realm of consciousness. Consciousness is merely vibration driven by survival, and obsession is only a system activation state when resources are present.

Huzi’s simplicity, loyalty, and purity are not foolishness — they are the most authentic form of life. With the most basic logic of survival, he lifts the fog at the core of consciousness. Humans have overcomplicated consciousness, intelligence, and spirituality, granting them excessive privilege and superiority. Yet in the daily life of a rural dog, all these so-called advanced traits are reduced to the most primitive survival vibrations.

Buddha’s great wisdom is cultivated non-attachment; Huzi’s half-wisdom is innate non-attachment. The external manifestations of the two are completely equivalent. This is the ultimate conclusion I derived from Huzi: Great Wisdom = Half Wisdom.

He is the primary living data for my theories, a living witness to the origin of consciousness, and a crucial source of all my inspiration. This subversive understanding of consciousness and intelligence cannot be separated from Huzi’s daily, vivid presence. He is more than just my dog — he is the most special co-creator in this revolution of consciousness.

一个真实的故事，其意义远远超过故事本身

----------------------

我的狗 “虎子”，推导大智 = 半智

----------------------

我所有关于意识与智能的本源理论，最核心、最鲜活的锚点，并非来自艰深的论文、精密的实验，而是来自我从小养到大的土狗 —— 虎子。可以说，OFIRM 体系的底层逻辑，一大半都是在遛虎子、观察它与同伴相处的日常里，一点点推演出来的。

虎子是我亲手接生、抱在怀里养大的，如今长得壮实，早已抱不动了。它不算聪明，很多技能学不会，智识水平充其量只有 0.5，满脑子都是最朴素的生存意识，没有复杂的自我算计，更没有人类的执念与纠结，可它的行为模式，却完美契合了佛陀修行一生才抵达的境界。也正是从它身上，我推导出了一个颠覆认知的结论：大智，等价于半智。

我养了三只狗，从前总给它们分盆喂食，以为能相安无事，结果根本没用。虎子天生要当老大，吃完自己的，立刻就去抢另外两只的食物，互不相让时甚至会撕咬到腿都流血，应激状态拉满，浑身的毛都炸着，满是攻击性。后来我索性改变策略，把狗粮一把撒开，让它们一起抢食，至少开局每只都能先吃饱，只剩最后几颗时才会出现争抢。

可最神奇的一幕永远发生在最后：当最后一颗狗粮被吃完，虎子和同伴们确认没有任何食物剩余，前一秒还剑拔弩张、咬出血的争斗，下一秒就彻底平息。应激瞬间消散，炸起的毛立刻平复，三只狗仿佛什么都没发生过，互相舔毛、亲昵依偎，全然没有记仇、没有怨念、没有心结。

这种极致的 “事过即空”，让我第一次把虎子和佛陀联系在了一起。

佛陀苦修一世，修的是放下执念，应无所住，不起波澜。欢喜来了便欢喜，纷争起了便淡然，事过之后心无挂碍，不被情绪与执念裹挟。而虎子天生如此，它的智识只有 0.5，没有复杂的思维缠绕，没有人类的贪嗔痴慢疑，却活成了佛陀的模样。

有食物、有资源时，它的生存意识被激活，震荡拉满，要抢、要争、要当老大，这是生存本能驱动的必然应激；无食物、无资源时，生存震荡瞬间归零，执念彻底消散，不记仇、不纠结、不内耗，瞬间回归平和与友善。

人类穷其一生追求的 “大智”，不过是主动剥离执念、回归本心；而虎子凭借 0.5 的半智，天生就具备这种特质。它没有复杂的自我意识，一切行为都围绕生存意识展开，不添多余的思虑，不造无谓的执念，恰恰印证了意识的本质：智慧高低从来不等同于意识境界，意识不过是生存驱动的震荡波动，执念只是资源在场时的系统激活状态。

虎子的傻乎乎、忠诚、纯粹，不是愚笨，而是生命最本真的样子。它用最简单的生存逻辑，揭开了意识最核心的迷雾 —— 人类把意识、智能、灵性想得太过神圣复杂，赋予了太多特权与优越感，可在一只土狗的日常里，这些所谓的高级特质，都被还原成了最朴素的生存震荡。

佛陀的大智，是修来的 “无执”；虎子的半智，是天生的 “无执”。二者外在表现完全等价，这便是我从虎子身上推导的终极结论：大智 = 半智。

它是我理论的第一手活体资料，是意识本源的活见证，更是我所有灵感的重要来源。这份对意识与智能的颠覆性认知，离不开虎子日复一日的鲜活呈现，它不仅是我的狗，更是这场意识革命里，最特殊的共创者。
