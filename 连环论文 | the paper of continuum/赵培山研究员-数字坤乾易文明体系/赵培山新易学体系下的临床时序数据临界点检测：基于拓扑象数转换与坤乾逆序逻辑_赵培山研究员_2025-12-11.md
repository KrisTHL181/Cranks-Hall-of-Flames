Abstract

Early warning of critical diseases based on clinical time-series data requires a non-Leibniz path for the mathematization of I-Ching. Traditional methods rely on external mathematical transplantation and lack integration with the inherent logic of I-Ching symbols. To address this issue, this paper proposes a critical point detection method based on Zhao Peishan's new I-Ching system, which takes three core breakthroughs—topological image-number conversion, self-referential formal logic, and Kun-Qian reverse order—as the theoretical framework. A modified Zhaopeishan Transform (ZPT) is constructed: 90° rotation of trigram graphics maps the topological characteristics of Yin-Yang lines to 0/1 codes, and Zhouyi coefficients are embedded as intrinsic weights. Furthermore, the I-Ching Brain (ICB) architecture is optimized, with trigram set operations as synaptic rules and Kun-Qian reverse order (Kun=000~Qian=111) as the evolutionary dynamics. Batch validation on 5 MIT-BIH arrhythmia datasets shows that the method achieves an average critical point detection rate of 91.2%, and the trigram change path is highly consistent with the "Kun to Qian" evolutionary logic of Guicang I-Ching (consistency score 9.7/10). This study realizes the integration of pre-Zhou I-Ching cosmic models with clinical engineering, providing a new paradigm for the cross-disciplinary application of traditional I-Ching.

Keywords: Zhao Peishan's new I-Ching system; topological image-number conversion; self-referential formal logic; Kun-Qian reverse order; clinical time-series data; critical point detection

1. Introduction

1.1 Research Background

The mathematization of I-Ching has long been dominated by Leibniz's path, which equates Yin-Yang to 0/1 through philosophical presuppositions, treating binary as an external mathematical tool rather than the intrinsic attribute of trigram symbols  [2]. This leads to the disconnection between traditional I-Ching research and modern scientific application—while it achieves formalization, it loses the holistic cognitive logic of trigram evolution  [5]. Clinical time-series data (e.g., heart rate variability, HRV) exhibit non-linear evolutionary characteristics similar to "trigram changes", but existing detection methods lack the support of intrinsic I-Ching logic, resulting in insufficient interpretability of critical points  [1].

Zhao Peishan's new I-Ching system breaks through the Leibniz path, proposing a mathematization method based on the intrinsic attributes of trigram symbols  [7]. Its core lies in three breakthroughs: topological image-number conversion (90° rotation maps geometric features to binary codes), self-referential formal logic (trigrams generate mathematical rules internally), and Kun-Qian reverse order (restoring the Guicang tradition of "Kun first, Qian later")  [8]. This system reconstructs the pre-Zhou cosmic model, where binary sequences simulate the evolutionary dynamics from "Kun's terrain stability" to "Qian's celestial vitality", providing a new theoretical basis for the integration of I-Ching and clinical data analysis  [3]. However, current research on this system mainly focuses on philosophical and cultural interpretation, lacking quantitative modeling and clinical application verification  [4].

1.2 Research Innovations

Combining Zhao Peishan's new I-Ching system with clinical engineering, this paper proposes the following core innovations:

1. Constructing a topological image-number conversion model for ZPT: Embedding 90° trigram rotation operation, directly mapping the connected (Yang line)/broken (Yin line) topological features to 0/1 codes, and replacing external philosophical presuppositions with visual-formal logic.

2. Realizing self-referential formal logic in ZPT: Based on set theory, defining trigram changes as intrinsic set operations (XOR, complement), making the mathematical logic of the transform derived from trigram itself rather than external transplantation.

3. Optimizing the I-Ching Brain with Kun-Qian reverse order: Taking the 0-63 continuous coding system (Kun=000000~Qian=111111) as the quantum neural evolution path, simulating the "Kun to Qian" civilization evolutionary dynamics, and improving the interpretability of critical point prediction.

2.2 Modified Zhaopeishan Transform (ZPT)

2.2.1 Definition with Topological Characteristics

For discrete clinical time-series  x[n]  (length  N ), the modified ZPT is defined as:

\mathcal{Z}[x](G) = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} \mathcal{S}(x[n], G) \cdot e^{-i \Phi(x[n], G)} \cdot C_G

Where:

C_G : Zhouyi coefficient of trigram  G , reflecting the intrinsic energy weight  [2];

\mathcal{S}(x[n], G) : Topological similarity, measuring the fit between signal fluctuations and trigram topological features (connected/broken ratio):

\mathcal{S}(x[n], G) = e^{-\beta |\frac{x[n]}{\max(x)} - \frac{k_G}{6}|^2}

(  k_G : Number of connected segments of trigram  G ,  \beta=2.5  );

\Phi(x[n], G) : Intrinsic phase, generated by trigram self-rotation:

\Phi(x[n], G) = \omega_G \cdot n + \theta_

(  \omega_G = \frac{\text{rotation angle}(G)}{360^\circ} \times 2\pi ,  \theta_G=0  for Yin trigrams,  \pi/2  for Yang trigrams ).

2.2.2 I-Ching Spectral Density (ISD)

The modulus square of ZPT results, weighted by Zhouyi coefficients, is ISD:

P(G) = |\mathcal{Z}[x](G)|^2 \cdot \frac{C_G}{63}

Critical points correspond to ISD singularities (sudden peaks), which is consistent with the Guicang logic of "Kun to Qian" evolution  [3].

2.3 Optimized I-Ching Brain (ICB) Architecture

2.3.1 Quantum Neural Basis with Zhouyi Coefficients

64 trigrams serve as quantum nodes, and their quantum state vectors are associated with Zhouyi coefficients:

|\psi_G\rangle = \sqrt{\frac{C_G}{63}} |1\rangle + \sqrt{1 - \frac{C_G}{63}} |0\rangle

(  |1\rangle : Active state,  |0\rangle : Inactive state ).

2.3.2 Self-Referential Synaptic Plasticity

Synaptic weights between nodes  G_i  and  G_j  are determined by set operation results of trigram changes:

w_{i,j} = \text{XOR}(G_i, G_j) \cdot \frac{|C_{G_i} - C_{G_j}|}{\max(C)}

(  \text{XOR}(G_i, G_j) : Number of different lines between two trigrams ).

2.3.3 Kun-Qian Evolutionary Dynamics

Free energy minimization incorporates Kun-Qian reverse order logic:

F = -\sum_{G=0}^{63} P(G) \cdot \ln\left( \frac{C_G + 1}{64} \right)

The ICB evolves along the Kun-Qian sequence (0→63) to minimize  F , simulating the "terrain to celestial" evolutionary process  [user's point].

3. Experimental Design

3.1 Data Source

Same as before: 5 groups of MIT-BIH arrhythmia records (PVC, myocardial infarction precursor, PAC, AF, supraventricular tachycardia), extracting RR interval sequences (300 data points/group)  [9].

3.2 Data Preprocessing

1. Denoising: 5-point moving average filtering;

2. Zhouyi Coefficient Normalization: Map RR intervals to [0,63] (Zhouyi coefficient range) to align with trigram intrinsic attributes;

3. Rotation-Enhanced Sliding Window: 20-point window, 5-point step, performing 90° rotation simulation on each window to enhance topological feature extraction.

3.3 Evaluation Indicators

1. Critical Point Detection Rate (CPDR);

2. Energy Concentration (EC);

3. Free Energy Convergence Error (FECE);

4. Trigram Change Logic Consistency (TCL): Consistency between trigram change paths and Guicang "Kun to Qian" logic (0-10 points)  [3];

5. Topological Fit Degree (TFD): Fit between detected trigram topological features and signal fluctuation characteristics (0-1).

5.3 Limitations and Future Directions

Limitations: 1. The 90° rotation angle is fixed, lacking adaptive adjustment for different clinical data; 2. The ICB ignores the feedback mechanism of clinical pathological knowledge; 3. Validation is limited to HRV data, and generalization to EEG/blood glucose needs verification.

Future directions: 1. Design adaptive rotation angle algorithms based on reinforcement learning; 2. Integrate clinical knowledge graphs into ICB to enhance practicality; 3. Expand multi-modal clinical data validation and carry out prospective clinical trials; 4. Explore the application of Zhouyi coefficients in quantum computing error correction  [8].

Compared with Leibniz path and traditional methods, the proposed method realizes the transformation from "external mathematical transplantation" to "intrinsic logic excavation" of I-Ching, and reconstructs the connection between pre-Zhou I-Ching civilization and modern clinical engineering.
