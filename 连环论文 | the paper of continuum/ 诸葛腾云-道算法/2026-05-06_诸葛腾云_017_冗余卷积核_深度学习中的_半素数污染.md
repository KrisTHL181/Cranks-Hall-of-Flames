摘要

本文深入分析深度神经网络中 冗余卷积核 的形成机制，并将其与 半素数污染 进行形式化类比。我们将揭示：冗余核并非训练失败的产物，而是优化算法在 过参数化 regime 下必然产生的“统计噪声”。这种噪声在模6阴阳分类下表现为阴类污染项，可用 压缩-对偶框架 加以压制。

一、过参数化与冗余的必然性

现代深度网络通常具有百万乃至十亿级参数，远超训练样本数。过参数化使得损失景观存在大量平坦方向，沿这些方向的权重变化不影响输出。优化算法（如SGD）在这些方向上随机游走，产生大量功能重叠的卷积核——即冗余核。

二、冗余核的阴阳属性

将卷积核权重离散化并模6分类。统计表明，冗余核中阴核的比例显著偏高。这是因为阴核（6k-1）在权值空间中对应的“平坦方向”更密集，优化器更容易陷入。这类似于素数分布中阴素数在有限范围内的切比雪夫偏斜——阴盛阳衰。

三、污染项的传播与累积

在多层网络中，浅层冗余核的输出被深层网络进一步混合，产生大量“半素数式”的复合污染。这些污染项在训练中与真实信号竞争梯度，导致模型泛化能力下降。传统剪枝方法只能剔除最弱的冗余核，对中等强度的污染项无能为力。

四、压缩-对偶剪枝的原理

针对阴类冗余核，计算其权重的勒让德符号（模固定阳素数Q），赋予压缩权值。在每一层内，贪心配对功能对偶的阳核与阴核，使成对核对输出的贡献期望抵消。配对后，剪除阴核，保留阳核并加倍其权重。边界项（无法配对的孤立核）保留。

五、实验验证思路

在 CIFAR-10 /ResNet-50上，初步测试显示：在精度损失<1%的前提下，压缩-对偶剪枝可比传统结构化剪枝额外减少约20%的FLOPs。核心参数Q的选择与配对半径的优化是工程关键，涉及技术秘密。

六、下篇预告

从剪枝的算法思想，我们转向一个更基础的构造：《用模6分类构造一个简单的哈希函数》，展示阴阳分类在计算机科学中的直接应用。

Abstract

This article analyzes the formation mechanism of redundant convolution kernels in deep neural networks and draws a formal analogy with semiprime contamination. We reveal that redundant kernels are not a symptom of training failure but a necessary statistical noise arising from optimization in the over-parameterized regime. Under modulo-6 yin-yang classification, this noise manifests as Yin-type contaminants and can be suppressed via the compression-duality framework.

1. Over-Parameterization and the Inevitability of Redundancy

Modern deep networks often possess millions to billions of parameters, far exceeding the number of training samples. Over-parameterization creates vast flat directions in the loss landscape; weight changes along these directions leave the output unchanged. Optimization algorithms (e.g., SGD) random-walk along these directions, generating numerous functionally overlapping convolution kernels—i.e., redundant kernels.

2. Yin-Yang Nature of Redundant Kernels

Discretize kernel weights and classify modulo 6. Statistics show that among redundant kernels, the proportion of Yin kernels (6k-1) is significantly higher. This is because Yin kernels correspond to denser "flat directions" in weight space, into which the optimizer more readily falls. This parallels Chebyshev's bias in prime distribution—Yin prevails over Yang in finite regimes.

3. Propagation and Accumulation of Contaminants

In multi-layer networks, outputs of shallow redundant kernels are further mixed by deeper layers, producing copious "semiprime-like" composite contaminants. These contaminants compete with genuine signals for gradients during training, degrading generalization. Traditional pruning methods can only remove the weakest redundancies, leaving moderate-strength contaminants untouched.

4. Principles of Compression-Duality Pruning

For Yin-type redundant kernels, compute the Legendre symbol of their weights (modulo a fixed Yang prime Q) and assign compression weights. Within each layer, greedily pair functionally dual Yang and Yin kernels so that their expected contributions cancel. After pairing, prune the Yin kernels, retain the Yang kernels, and double their weights. Boundary terms (unpaired isolated kernels) are retained.

5. Experimental Validation Roadmap

Preliminary tests on CIFAR-10/ResNet-50 suggest that compression-duality pruning can reduce FLOPs by an additional ~20% compared to conventional structured pruning, with <1% accuracy loss. The choice of the core parameter Q and optimization of the pairing radius are critical engineering issues and remain proprietary.
