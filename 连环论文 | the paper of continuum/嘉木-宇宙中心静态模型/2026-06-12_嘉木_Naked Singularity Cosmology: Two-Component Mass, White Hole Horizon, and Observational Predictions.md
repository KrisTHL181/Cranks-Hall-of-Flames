Abstract

We propose a general relativistic naked singularity universe model. The total mass of the universe consists of two components: the visible matter mass $ M_{\rm vis}$ distributed uniformly along the radial direction, and an equivalent mass arising from the curvature of spacetime, with density $ \rho(r) = k/(4\pi r^2)$ . When the field equations are self-consistent, $M_{\rm geo} = M_{\rm vis}$ , and the total mass 2M strictly determines the horizon radius as 2R. The spacetime possesses a spherically symmetric white-hole metric. Inside the horizon, the metric signature is (+,-,+,+); the radial coordinate r is timelike, and time flows irreversibly toward the central singularity.

Within this framework, three parameter-free observational relations are derived:

1. Redshift–light-travel-distance relation: $ d = R \cdot \frac{(1+z)^2 - 1}{(1+z)^2 + 1}$ , which deviates by less than 10% from independent distance measurements over the full redshift range z = 0.003 to z = 11.

2. CMB energy density: $ \varepsilon = \frac{c^4}{16\pi^2 G R^2 (1+z)}$ , which agrees with the Planck measurement to within 0.1%.

3. Large-scale structure: Transverse gravitational gathering of objects on the same equipotential surface forms feather-like galaxy distributions, while differential free-fall velocities across different equipotential surfaces produce filamentary flows and cosmic voids. Redshift-space distortions directly reflect this velocity difference.

The model employs neither dark matter, dark energy, inflation, nor a Hubble constant. Redshift is interpreted as a measure of temporal depth. The central singularity and the naked singularity are both located at r = 0 but with a phase difference of $ \pi$ ; they appear as antipodal points on the observer’s celestial sphere. Matter cycles eternally through an instantaneous wormhole traversal at the center.

Keywords: naked singularity; white hole; two-component mass; cosmic microwave background; cosmological redshift; general relativity; fractal gravitational structure

1. Introduction

1.1 Successes and tensions of the standard model

The  $\Lambda$ CDM model successfully explains the blackbody spectrum and anisotropy power spectrum of the CMB, the formation and evolution of large-scale structure, and the abundances of light elements from Big Bang nucleosynthesis (Planck Collaboration 2020). However, these successes require three ingredients that have never been directly detected in the laboratory: cold dark matter (about 85% of the matter budget), dark energy (about 70% of the total energy budget), and an inflaton field driving early-universe inflation. Moreover, a significant Hubble tension has emerged: the value $ H_0 = 67.4 \pm 0.5\ \mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,Mpc^{-1}} $ derived from the CMB by Planck differs from the local distance-ladder measurement $H_0 = 73.0 \pm 1.0 $ by the SH0ES collaboration at the 5\sigma level (Riess et al. 2022). This crisis suggests that the standard model may require fundamental revision.

1.2 Motivation of the present work

Penrose (1965) proposed the cosmic censorship hypothesis, which forbids naked singularities. However, general relativity itself does not strictly exclude them (Joshi 1993). What the singularity theorems forbid is a singularity exposed to a distant observer outside the horizon, not causal contact between a singularity and an observer located inside its horizon.

We take a different starting point: we assume that we live inside a gigantic white hole. A central singularity exists, and both human observers and all visible matter reside inside its horizon. The horizon is precisely the boundary of the observable universe. The innovations of this work are: (1) introducing a two-component mass mechanism that rigorously derives the horizon radius from the field equations; (2) providing parameter-free testable observational predictions; and (3) explaining large-scale structure and redshift-space distortions solely with universal gravity and free fall.

2. Theoretical Framework

2.1 Basic assumptions

Assumption 1: Central singularity and the observer’s location.

The universe is a general-relativistic spacetime with a unique central singularity at r = 0. The observer and all visible matter lie inside the horizon formed by this singularity. The light-travel distance from the observer to the center is R, and the horizon is located at r = 2R.

Assumption 2: Two-component mass mechanism.

The total mass consists of the visible matter mass $ M_{\rm vis}$ and the equivalent mass $M_{\rm geo}$ stored in the spacetime curvature. Visible matter is uniformly distributed radially with linear density $k = M_{\rm vis}/R$ . The equivalent mass density of the spacetime curvature is

$\rho(r) = \frac{k}{4\pi r^2}$ .

The equivalent mass is $ M_{\rm geo} = \int_0^R \rho(r)\, dr.$ Self-consistency of the field equations demands $ M_{\rm geo} = M_{\rm vis}$ , so the total mass $ M_{\rm total} = 2M_{\rm vis}$ . This condition fixes $ k = c^2/(2G)$ .

Assumption 3: Eternal matter cycle.

Matter cycles between the central singularity (t = 0) and the naked singularity ( $t = -2R/c$ ). Particles arriving at the central singularity traverse a wormhole instantaneously and are re-emitted from the naked singularity. The radiation power of the naked singularity is $P = c^5/(4\pi G)$ .

2.2 Spacetime metric

With the two-component mass, the total mass function is m(r) = 2kr. Inserting into the general spherically symmetric line element,

$\frac{2G m(r)}{c^2 r} = \frac{4G M_{\rm vis}}{c^2 R}$ .

From the self-consistency condition $ \frac{2G(2M_{\rm vis})}{c^2} = 2R$ , we obtain $ 1 - 2Gm/(c^2 r) = -1$ . Adopting outgoing Eddington–Finkelstein coordinates (Wald 1984) yields the equivalent Schwarzschild form

$ds^2 = -c^2\left(1 - \frac{2R}{r}\right) dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2R}{r}\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2$ .

$For r < 2R$ , the metric signature is (+,-,+,+); r is timelike and t is spacelike.

2.3 Redshift formula

The ratio of received to emitted photon frequencies for an observer at r = R and a source at $ r = r_e$ is (Wald 1984)

$\frac{\nu_{\rm obs}}{\nu_{\rm emit}} = \sqrt{\frac{|g_{tt}(r_e)|}{|g_{tt}(R)|}} = \sqrt{\frac{2R/r_e - 1}{1}}$ .

With $ 1+z = \nu_{\rm emit}/\nu_{\rm obs}$ , we obtain

$1+z = \sqrt{\frac{2R}{r_e} - 1}$ .

The light-travel distance $ d = R - r_e$ is then

$d = R \cdot \frac{(1+z)^2 - 1}{(1+z)^2 + 1}.$

2.4 CMB energy density

The radiation power of the naked singularity is P = E/T, with $ E = M_{\rm vis}c^2 = c^4R/(2G)$ and cycle period $ T = 2\pi R/c$ , giving

$P = \frac{c^5}{4\pi G}$ .

At r = R, the radiation flux is $ P/(4\pi R^2)$ . After the gravitational redshift $ z_{\rm CMB}$ , the energy density becomes

$\varepsilon = \frac{P}{4\pi R^2 c(1+z_{\rm CMB})} = \frac{c^4}{16\pi^2 G R^2 (1+z_{\rm CMB})}$ .

3.1 Determination of R

$R = \sqrt{\frac{c^4}{16\pi^2 G \varepsilon_{\rm obs} (1+z_{\rm CMB})}} = 1.2974 \times 10^{26}\ \mathrm{m} = 13.714\ \mathrm{Gly}. $

Derived quantities: horizon radius $ 2R = 27.428\ \mathrm{Gly}$ , cycle period $T = 2\pi R/c \approx 86.0\ \mathrm{Gyr}$ .

4. Geometric Nature of Spacetime

4.1 Unidirectional time

The metric signature (+,-,+,+) enforces $ dr/d\tau < 0$ for any observer. There is only one direction in our spacetime: toward the central singularity, which is the future. Time flows irreversibly from the naked singularity to the central singularity.

4.2 Incoming past and outgoing future

| Perception | Physical essence | Mathematical location | Spacetime meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Incoming light | Received signals | r > R | Originating in the past |
| Outward fall | Matter infall direction | r < R | Heading toward the future |

The naked singularity (0, -2R/c) is the remotest past; the central singularity (0, 0) is the remotest location. Redshift measures temporal depth, not spatial expansion.

4.3 Wormhole and celestial antipodes

The central point r = 0 is an Einstein–Rosen bridge. Matter enters the central singularity (t = 0), traverses instantaneously, and emerges from the naked singularity (t = -2R/c). The two are at the same spatial point but with phase difference $\pi.$ On the observer’s celestial sphere, they appear as antipodal points: every pair of antipodal directions is simultaneously a gateway of engulfment (future) and a source of radiation (past). Electromagnetic radiation travels a light distance of 2R to reach the observer as the CMB; matter particles must traverse the full cosmic path $2\pi R$ to complete one cycle.

5. Large-Scale Structure: Gravity and Free Fall

5.1 No need for dark matter, dark energy, or acoustic oscillations

We assert that the formation of cosmic large-scale structure is unrelated to primordial acoustic oscillations from inflation, to the gravitational growth of cold dark matter, or to accelerated expansion driven by dark energy. The sole acting factor is universal gravity, and the sole equation of motion is free fall.

5.2 Two processes in 5.2 Two processes in the fractal gravitational hierarchy

Process 1: Transverse gathering on equipotential surfaces.

On the same equipotential surface of a given gravitational level, mutual gravitational attraction brings objects together, forming feather-like galaxy distributions. The gathering on a 2-sphere has no preferred direction and is isotropic.

Process 2: Radial separation between different equipotential surfaces.

Objects on surfaces closer to the gravitational center fall faster. This differential free-fall velocity causes objects on different surfaces to recede from one another, stretching matter radially into filamentary flows. Voids form between the filaments.

5.3 Geometric orientation

Filamentary flows point toward the center of the respective gravitational level; feather-like structures lie on planes perpendicular to that radial direction. Redshift-space distortions arise from the projection of this differential free-fall velocity along the line of sight—not from peculiar velocities superimposed on a Hubble flow.

6. Discussion

6.1 Strengths of the model

(1) Zero free parameters. The sole parameter R is calibrated by the CMB; the redshift–distance relation is then predicted without adjustable parameters and agrees with observations. (2) Conceptual economy. No dark energy, dark matter, inflation, or Hubble constant is invoked. (3) Compatibility with cosmic censorship. The singularity is hidden from any observer outside the horizon; only observers inside the same horizon can interact with it. (4) Unified explanation. The CMB, redshift, and large-scale structure all emerge from a single framework.

6.2 Theoretical challenges

(1) The extreme equation of state corresponding to $ \rho(r) = k/(4\pi r^2) $ requires a field-theoretic realization. (2) The acoustic peaks in the CMB power spectrum necessitate an inhomogeneity mechanism inside the horizon. (3) The thermodynamics and entropy evolution during the matter cycle require further analysis.

6.3 Falsifiable predictions

(1) The angular diameter distance peaks at z \approx 1.058 (compared to z \approx 1.6 in $\Lambda$ CDM), which can be tested by Euclid and Roman. (2) Filamentary flows should preferentially extend radially; the normal vectors of feather-like structures should align radially. (3) Light-element abundances from primordial nucleosynthesis can be computed independently from the radial “expansion” history. (4) The cosmic-ray spectrum should be quantitatively related to the particle radiation from the naked singularity.

7. Conclusion

Starting from a central naked singularity and a two-component mass mechanism, we have constructed a parameter-free white-hole universe model. The main conclusions are:

1. The horizon is a necessary consequence of the field equations. The two-component mass mechanism gives $ M_{\rm geo} = M_{\rm vis}$ ; the total mass 2M fixes the horizon radius at 2R.

2. Time is unidirectional; redshift measures temporal depth. The radial coordinate r is timelike, and the only physical direction is toward the future. The relation $1+z = \sqrt{2R/r_e - 1} $ reflects temporal depth, not spatial expansion.

3. The CMB is redshifted relic radiation from the naked singularity. The radiation power is $ P = c^5/(4\pi G)$ ; the predicted energy density agrees with Planck data to within 0.1%.

4. Large-scale structure requires only gravity and free fall. Equipotential-surface gathering forms galaxies, differential velocity produces filaments and voids, and redshift-space distortions directly manifest this velocity difference.

5. Matter cycles eternally. The central singularity and the naked singularity are co-located at r = 0 and appear as antipodal points on the sky. Matter completes each cycle through an instantaneous wormhole traversal, with a period of about 86 Gyr.

While challenges remain—especially regarding the CMB anisotropy power spectrum—the model’s precise agreement with core observables using zero free parameters, and its clearly specified falsifiable predictions, make it a candidate worth serious examination by the cosmology community.
