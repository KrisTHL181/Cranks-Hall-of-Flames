摘要

压缩算子的核心是权函数ω=½(1-χ(p+))，其中χ为勒让德符号。本文解释为何该算子具有“不可伪造性”：任何人若不能分解半素数n，就无法以显著优势计算χ(p+)。这一性质植根于二次剩余的困难性，是压缩-对偶框架在密码学中应用的基石。

一、二次剩余问题

给定奇素数Q和整数a，判断a是否为模Q的二次剩余，存在多项式时间算法（计算勒让德符号）。但若给定合数n=pq（p,q为素数）和整数a，判断a是否为模n的二次剩余，在不知p,q的情况下被认为是困难的。这就是二次剩余假设。

二、压缩算子的单向性

压缩算子ω(n)需要计算χ(p+)，其中p+是半素数n的阳因子。已知n而不知p+时，计算χ(p+)等价于求解二次剩余问题模p+——而p+未知。这构成了一个陷门：知道p+的人容易计算ω，不知道的人无法计算。因此，压缩算子是一个候选的单向函数。

三、与RSA的比较

RSA的安全性基于大整数分解。压缩算子的单向性同样基于分解，但更精细：它只要求提取阳因子的二次剩余特征，而非完整分解。理论上，这可能比完整分解略易，但当前尚无多项式时间算法能仅凭n判断χ(p+)。因此，压缩算子在复杂度上与RSA相当。

四、不可伪造性的应用

若将压缩算子用于数字签名或身份认证：证明者知道p+，可快速计算ω并生成证明；验证者只知道n，无法伪造证明。这为轻量级认证协议提供了新的数论原语。具体协议设计涉及技术秘密，本文不展开。

Abstract

The core of the compression operator is the weight function ω = ½(1-χ(p+)), where χ is the Legendre symbol. This article explains why this operator is "unforgeable": anyone who cannot factor the semiprime n cannot compute χ(p+) with significant advantage. This property is rooted in the hardness of quadratic residuosity and underpins cryptographic applications of the compression-duality framework.

1. The Quadratic Residuosity Problem

Given an odd prime Q and an integer a, determining whether a is a quadratic residue modulo Q admits a polynomial-time algorithm (computing the Legendre symbol). However, given a composite n = pq (p,q primes) and an integer a, deciding whether a is a quadratic residue modulo n is believed hard without knowing p and q. This is the quadratic residuosity assumption.

2. One-Wayness of the Compression Operator

The compression operator ω(n) requires computing χ(p+), where p+ is the Yang factor of the semiprime n. Knowing n but not p+, computing χ(p+) is equivalent to solving the quadratic residuosity problem modulo p+—with p+ unknown. This creates a trapdoor: whoever knows p+ can easily compute ω; whoever does not, cannot. Thus, the compression operator is a candidate one-way function.

3. Comparison with RSA

RSA's security rests on integer factorization. The one-wayness of the compression operator also rests on factorization, but is more refined: it requires only extracting the quadratic residue characteristic of the Yang factor, not full factorization. In theory, this might be slightly easier than complete factorization, yet no polynomial-time algorithm is known to decide χ(p+) from n alone. Hence, the compression operator is complexity-theoretically comparable to RSA.

4. Applications of Unforgeability

If the compression operator were used in digital signatures or identity authentication: a prover knowing p+ can quickly compute ω and generate a proof; a verifier knowing only n cannot forge the proof. This offers a new number-theoretic primitive for lightweight authentication protocols. Specific protocol designs involve proprietary details and are omitted here.
