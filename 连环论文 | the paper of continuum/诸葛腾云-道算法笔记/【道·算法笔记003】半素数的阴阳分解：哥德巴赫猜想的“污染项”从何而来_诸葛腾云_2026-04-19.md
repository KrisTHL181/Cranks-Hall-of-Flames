**摘要**

**在前两篇中，我们确立了素数的模6阴阳分类。本文进一步考察两个素数的乘积——半素数——的阴阳属性。我们将看到，半素数的阴阳分解，正是哥德巴赫猜想与孪生素数猜想中“污染项”的数论根源。本文为后续引入“压缩算子”提供动机铺垫，不涉及核心算法参数。**

**一、半素数：素数的“影子”**

**两个素数相乘，得到的数称为半素数（semiprime）。例如：15=3×5，21=3×7，35=5×7，143=11×13。半素数不是素数，却由两个素数因子构成，因此常出现在与素数分布相关的筛法中，成为最难剔除的“污染项”。**

**在哥德巴赫猜想中，我们试图将一个偶数表示为两个素数之和。但当我们用筛法筛选时，大量半素数伪装成“几乎素数”，干扰计数。要理解这种污染的本质，必须看清半素数的阴阳结构。**

**二、半素数的模6阴阳分类**

**根据因子类型，半素数可分为三类（此处只讨论大于3的素因子）：**

**1. 阳×阳：两个阳素数相乘。例如7×13=91，91≡1(mod6)，乘积仍为阳数（6k+1）。**

**2. 阴×阴：两个阴素数相乘。例如5×11=55，55≡1(mod6)，乘积竟也是阳数。**

**3. 阳×阴：一个阳素数和一个阴素数相乘。例如5×7=35，35≡5(mod6)，乘积为阴数（6k-1）。**

**总结规律：**

**· 阳×阳 → 阳**

**· 阴×阴 → 阳**

**· 阳×阴 → 阴**

**这个乘法表，与“负负得正”的直觉完全一致。若将阳素数记为+1，阴素数记为-1，则半素数的“阴阳符”恰为两因子符号之积。**

**三、污染项的真面目**

**在哥德巴赫分拆的筛法中，我们期望计数的是“真素数对”。但筛法无法轻易区分一个素数与一个“阳×阴”型半素数——因为它们在模6下的余数可能相同。更麻烦的是，“阳×阳”和“阴×阴”型半素数，虽然乘积是阳数，却在后续统计中反复出现，造成系统性的计数偏差。**

**如果用模6阴阳的语言重新描述：哥德巴赫分拆中的“污染项”，本质上是那些无法通过简单同余条件排除的半素数。它们的阴阳符号组合，恰好形成了一类“期望非零”的统计噪声。**

**四、压缩算子的动机**

**如何压制这些污染项？既然污染项有着特定的阴阳符号结构，我们能否构造一个权函数，使得阴类污染项的贡献在统计上期望为零？这正是下一篇《勒让德符号：如何用一个二次特征为整数“定性”》将要展开的内容。**

**我们将引入二次剩余（勒让德符号），为每个半素数的阳因子赋予一个±1的“特征值”。通过精心设计的权函数，阴类半素数的贡献将在求和中相互抵消——这便是“压缩算子”的数学雏形。**

**五、结语**

**半素数的阴阳分解，是连接素数分类与猜想证明的桥梁。它让我们看清了“污染”的结构，也为“抵消”提供了靶点。下一篇，我们将正式引入勒让德符号，迈出构造压缩算子的第一步。**

**Abstract**

**In the previous two articles, we established the modulo-6 yin-yang classification of primes. This article examines the yin-yang properties of semiprimes—products of two primes. We will see that the yin-yang decomposition of semiprimes lies at the heart of the "contamination" plaguing sieve methods for Goldbach's conjecture and the twin prime conjecture. This provides the motivation for the "compression operator" to be introduced later. Core parameters are withheld.**

**1. Semiprimes: The "Shadows" of Primes**

**The product of two primes is called a semiprime. Examples: 15=3×5, 21=3×7, 35=5×7, 143=11×13. Semiprimes are not prime, yet they are composed of prime factors. Consequently, they frequently appear as the most stubborn "contaminants" in sieves concerning prime distribution.**

**In Goldbach's conjecture, we attempt to represent an even number as the sum of two primes. However, when applying a sieve, numerous semiprimes masquerade as "almost-primes," distorting the count. To understand the nature of this contamination, we must examine the yin-yang structure of semiprimes.**

**2. Modulo-6 Yin-Yang Classification of Semiprimes**

**Based on the types of their factors, semiprimes (with prime factors >3) fall into three categories:**

**1. Yang × Yang: Product of two Yang primes. E.g., 7×13=91, 91≡1(mod6); the product remains a Yang number (6k+1).**

**2. Yin × Yin: Product of two Yin primes. E.g., 5×11=55, 55≡1(mod6); the product is, surprisingly, also a Yang number.**

**3. Yang × Yin: Product of a Yang prime and a Yin prime. E.g., 5×7=35, 35≡5(mod6); the product is a Yin number (6k-1).**

**In summary:**

**· Yang × Yang → Yang**

**· Yin × Yin → Yang**

**· Yang × Yin → Yin**

**This multiplication table aligns perfectly with the intuition that "a negative times a negative yields a positive." If we assign +1 to Yang primes and -1 to Yin primes, the "yin-yang sign" of a semiprime is simply the product of the signs of its factors.**

**3. The True Face of Contamination**

**In sieves for Goldbach partitions, we aim to count genuine prime pairs. However, the sieve struggles to distinguish a prime from a "Yang×Yin" semiprime—they may share the same residue modulo 6. Worse still, "Yang×Yang" and "Yin×Yin" semiprimes, though yielding Yang numbers, repeatedly introduce systematic biases in the counts.**

**In the language of modulo-6 yin-yang, the "contamination" in Goldbach's conjecture consists precisely of those semiprimes that cannot be eliminated by simple congruence conditions. Their specific yin-yang sign combinations constitute a form of statistical noise with non-zero expectation.**

**4. Motivation for the Compression Operator**

**How can we suppress these contaminants? Since the contaminants possess a specific yin-yang sign structure, can we construct a weight function such that the contribution of Yin-type contaminants has an expectation of zero in the relevant sums? This is the subject of the next article: The Legendre Symbol: How to Assign a "Character" to Integers Using Quadratic Residues.**

**We will introduce the quadratic residue (Legendre symbol) to assign a ±1 "character value" to the Yang factor of each semiprime. Through a carefully designed weight function, the contributions of Yin-type semiprimes will cancel each other out in summation—a prototype of the "compression operator."**

**5. Conclusion**

**The yin-yang decomposition of semiprimes serves as the bridge between prime classification and the proof of classical conjectures. It reveals the structure of the contamination and provides a target for cancellation. In the next article, we will formally introduce the Legendre symbol, taking the first step toward constructing the compression operator.**
