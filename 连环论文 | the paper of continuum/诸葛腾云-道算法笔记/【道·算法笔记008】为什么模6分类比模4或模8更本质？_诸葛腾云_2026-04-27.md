**摘要**

在前七篇中，我们反复使用模6分类将素数分为阳（6k+1）和阴（6k-1）。一个自然的问题是：为什么是模6，而不是模4或模8？本文从中国剩余定理出发，证明模6是唯一能同时“筛掉2和3的倍数、且保持阴阳对称”的模数。这一唯一性，是整个压缩-对偶框架的数学根基。

**一、模4为何不行？**

如果我们用模4分类，任意整数模4的余数属于{0,1,2,3}。大于2的素数必为奇数，所以余数只能是1或3。余1者（4k+1），余3者（4k-1）。这似乎也能将素数分成两类。

但模4有一个致命缺陷：它无法同时处理因子2和3。 模6天然排除了2和3的倍数（余0,2,3,4皆非素数），而模4只排除了2的倍数，3的倍数（如9,15,21）仍可能以余1或3的形式混入。这意味着，用模4分类时，“污染项”的类型更多，更难统一处理。此外，模4的二次剩余结构也不如模6丰富——模6与中国剩余定理深度绑定，模4则孤掌难鸣。

**二、模8为何也不行？**

模8的余数为0至7。大于2的素数必为奇数，余数可能为1,3,5,7。虽然模8也能筛掉2的倍数，但它引入了更多的余数类别——素数被分成了四类，而非两类。阴阳分类追求的是“二元对立统一”，四类则分散了统计抵消的效力。更重要的是，模8同样无法优雅地处理因子3。而模6恰好是最小的、能同时整除2和3的模数——这使得任何大于3的素数，在模6下只能落入两个“安全槽”：1和5。

**三、中国剩余定理的裁决**

中国剩余定理告诉我们：模6等价于模2和模3的联合作用。任何整数模6的余数，由它模2和模3的余数唯一确定。

· 模2排除偶数（余0）。

· 模3排除3的倍数（余0）。

两者联立，大于3的整数要想不被2和3整除，模6余数只能是与2互素且与3互素的数，即1和5。这就是模6分类唯一性的代数本质：它是唯一能同时以最小模数完成“去2去3”双筛的模数。 模4只有去2，模8只有去2，模12虽然也去2去3，但它是模6的倍数，余数类别更多（1,5,7,11），失去了二元对立的简洁性。

**四、阴阳对称的必然性**

模6不仅去除了2和3的污染，还恰好保留了两个余数：1和5。注意到5 ≡ -1 (mod 6)，所以这两个余数互为相反数。这正是阴阳对称的数学表达：阳（+1）与阴（-1），在模6的乘法群中构成一对互逆的元素。

这一对称性，是后续构造压缩算子（权函数依赖二次特征）和对偶映射（符号翻转）的先决条件。模4虽然也有1和3（3≡-1 mod 4），但它缺失了对因子3的筛除。模8的四个余数中，1和7互为相反数，3和5互为相反数，但对称性被稀释了。只有模6，在最小的模数下，同时实现了双筛与二元对称。

**五、结语与下篇预告**

模6不是随意选的，它是数学结构自身的选择——最小的、能同时筛除2和3、且余留两个互逆余数的模数。这一唯一性，为整个“阴阳算法”体系奠定了不可动摇的基石。下一篇，我们将用这个分类审视素数定理：《素数定理的阴阳视角：阳素数多还是阴素数多？》，看看阴阳素数在自然数中的真实比例。

**Abstract**

In the previous seven articles, we repeatedly used modulo-6 classification to partition primes into Yang (6k+1) and Yin (6k-1). A natural question arises: why modulo 6, and not modulo 4 or modulo 8? Starting from the Chinese Remainder Theorem, this article demonstrates that modulo 6 is the unique modulus that simultaneously "sieves out multiples of 2 and 3 while preserving yin-yang symmetry." This uniqueness is the mathematical foundation of the entire compression-duality framework.

**1. Why Modulo 4 Fails**

If we classify by modulo 4, any integer leaves a remainder in {0,1,2,3}. Primes greater than 2 must be odd, so the remainder can only be 1 or 3. Remainder 1 corresponds to 4k+1, and remainder 3 to 4k-1. This also seems to split primes into two classes.

However, modulo 4 has a fatal flaw: it cannot simultaneously handle the factors 2 and 3. Modulo 6 naturally excludes multiples of 2 and 3 (remainders 0,2,3,4 are all non-prime), whereas modulo 4 only excludes multiples of 2. Multiples of 3 (e.g., 9, 15, 21) may still sneak in as remainders 1 or 3. Consequently, under modulo-4 classification, the types of "contaminants" are more numerous and harder to handle uniformly. Moreover, the quadratic residue structure modulo 4 is not as rich as modulo 6—modulo 6 is deeply tied to the Chinese Remainder Theorem, while modulo 4 stands alone.

**2. Why Modulo 8 Also Fails**

Modulo 8 yields remainders 0 through 7. Primes greater than 2 are odd, so possible remainders are 1,3,5,7. Although modulo 8 also sieves out multiples of 2, it introduces more remainder classes—primes are split into four classes rather than two. The yin-yang classification seeks a "binary unity of opposites"; four classes dilute the efficacy of statistical cancellation. More importantly, modulo 8 also cannot elegantly handle the factor 3. Modulo 6 is precisely the smallest modulus that divides both 2 and 3—hence any prime greater than 3 can only fall into two "safe slots": 1 and 5.

**3. The Verdict of the Chinese Remainder Theorem**

The Chinese Remainder Theorem tells us that modulo 6 is equivalent to the joint action of modulo 2 and modulo 3. The remainder of any integer modulo 6 is uniquely determined by its remainders modulo 2 and modulo 3.

· Modulo 2 excludes even numbers (remainder 0).

· Modulo 3 excludes multiples of 3 (remainder 0).

Together, for an integer greater than 3 to be indivisible by 2 and 3, its remainder modulo 6 must be coprime to both 2 and 3—namely, 1 and 5. This is the algebraic essence of the uniqueness of modulo-6 classification: it is the unique smallest modulus that accomplishes the double sieve of "eliminating 2 and 3." Modulo 4 only eliminates 2; modulo 8 only eliminates 2; modulo 12 also eliminates 2 and 3, but being a multiple of 6, it yields more remainder classes (1,5,7,11), losing the elegant binary opposition.

**4. The Inevitability of Yin-Yang Symmetry**

Modulo 6 not only removes contamination by 2 and 3, but also leaves exactly two remainders: 1 and 5. Note that 5 ≡ -1 (mod 6), so these two remainders are mutual opposites. This is the mathematical expression of yin-yang symmetry: Yang (+1) and Yin (-1) form a pair of mutually inverse elements in the multiplicative group modulo 6.

This symmetry is a prerequisite for constructing both the compression operator (the weight function relies on quadratic characters) and the duality mapping (sign-flipping). Modulo 4 also has 1 and 3 (with 3 ≡ -1 mod 4), but it lacks the sieve for factor 3. Modulo 8 has four remainders; 1 and 7 are opposites, as are 3 and 5, but the symmetry is diluted. Only modulo 6, at the smallest modulus, achieves the double sieve and binary symmetry simultaneously.

**5. Conclusion and Preview**

Modulo 6 is not an arbitrary choice; it is the choice dictated by the mathematical structure itself—the smallest modulus that can simultaneously sieve out 2 and 3 while leaving two mutually inverse remainders. This uniqueness lays an unshakable foundation for the entire "yin-yang algorithm" framework.
