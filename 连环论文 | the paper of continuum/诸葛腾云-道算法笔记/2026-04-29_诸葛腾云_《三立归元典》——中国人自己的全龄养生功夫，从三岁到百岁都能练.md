《三立归元典》——中国人自己的全龄养生功夫，从三岁到百岁都能练

The Three Foundations Return to Origin Canon — China's Own All-Age Health Practice, from Age 3 to 103

【开头·我和这套功法的故事】

我叫诸葛腾云，本名陈滨杰，今年二十五岁。

三岁习武。六岁跆拳道。十二岁自由搏击。十六岁剑道。十九岁认识了我的剑道师父李炳哲。这些年学过的东西很多——传统武术、散打、泰拳、擒拿格斗，甚至研究过怎么破解女子防身术。但我从来没想过自己创一个流派。

直到我遇见了我的贵人罗公固。

他花了十年，磨出了一套道馆文化。他告诉我，我们不是做武道行业，我们是做教育。他把这套文化挂在墙上，让每一个进门的孩子第一眼就看到——

人欲立，正德而修身。国欲盛，崇文亦尚武。

人要在社会立足，必须先端正品德、修养身心。国家要强盛，不能只重文化教育，也要崇尚武术、强兵健体，文武并重，缺一不可。

这是道馆少年学员的集体誓言，谓之"公武训"。

他不只是教，他是活出来的。这么多年，他一个人在道馆里死磕，把单馆数据刷到极致，憋着一个"印钞机"的梦——先把一家馆做到最牛逼，再无限复制到全国、到全世界。可十年前他给别人讲这个梦，所有人都觉得他疯了。从那之后，他把这个梦咽回肚子里，谁也没再提。

直到他看到我。他发现我也在研究怎么让单馆做到最好。他怕我走他的老路，才把憋了十年的宏图大志告诉我。

那个晚上我失眠了。我想了一夜。我发现，市面上的功法，要么太难，普通人学不会；要么太玄，连我自己都不信；要么只适合某一个年龄段。孩子练不了老人的功，老人不敢练年轻人的功。

我要做一套所有人都能练的东西——从三岁能站桩的小孩，到九十岁坐着轮椅的老人，都能练，都有效。

我叫它《三立归元典》。

三立，来自我的贵人罗公固教我的公武训："首立德，次修身，终守恒"。归元，来自《黄帝内经》里的一句话："法于阴阳，和于术数"。而这一切的灵感，来自我生命中另一位贵人——我的剑道师父李炳哲。他送了我一串手串，十四颗金刚菩提，一颗玛瑙。阴阳交替，奇点贯通。

这是我的两位贵人。一个给了我"道"，一个给了我"器"。这套功法，是我对他的梦的交代，也是我对这个世界的交付。

I am Zhuge Tengyun, born Chen Binjie. I am twenty-five years old.

I started martial arts at three. Taekwondo at six. Kickboxing at twelve. Kendo at sixteen. At nineteen, I met my Kendo master Li Bingzhe. Over the years, I've trained in traditional Chinese martial arts, Sanda, Muay Thai, grappling, women's self-defense — and even studied how to counter women's self-defense. But I never imagined I would create my own school.

Until I met my mentor, Luo Gonggu.

He spent ten years forging a dojo culture. He told me: we are not in the martial arts business — we are in the business of education. He hung this culture on the wall, so every child who walks through the door sees it first —

If one wishes to stand firm, one must first rectify virtue and cultivate oneself. If a nation wishes to prosper, it must honor both culture and martial spirit.

This is the collective oath of the dojo's young students, called the "Gongwu Code."

He didn't just teach it. He lived it. For years, he grinded alone in a single dojo, optimizing every detail until it was flawless. He held onto a dream — to build one perfect dojo and then replicate it across the country and the world. But when he shared this dream ten years ago, everyone thought he was crazy. So he swallowed it. Never mentioned it again.

Until he saw me. He saw that I was also trying to figure out how to make a single dojo the best it could be. Afraid I'd walk the same lonely path he did, he finally told me his grand vision — a vision he'd kept locked away for ten years.

I couldn't sleep that night. I kept thinking: most existing practices are either too difficult for ordinary people, too mystical to be believable, or only suited for certain ages. Children can't do elder practices, and elders fear doing young people's routines.

I decided to create something that everyone can practice — from a three-year-old learning to stand, to a ninety-year-old in a wheelchair. Something that works for all.

I call it The Three Foundations Return to Origin Canon.

The "Three Foundations" come from the Gongwu Code taught to me by my mentor Luo Gonggu: "First establish virtue, then cultivate oneself, and ultimately persevere." The "Return to Origin" comes from the Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon: "Follow the rhythm of Yin and Yang, harmonize with the arts of health." And the spark of inspiration came from another crucial mentor in my life — my Kendo master Li Bingzhe. He gave me a bracelet: fourteen Bodhi seeds and one agate bead. Yin alternating with Yang, with a Singularity at the center.

These are my two mentors. One gave me the "Tao," the other gave me the "vessel." This practice is my answer to my mentor's dream, and my gift to the world.

【功法与公武训的对应】

这套功法与公武训一一对应：

· 预备式混元桩：站桩立根基 → 对应"立德"——知礼、感恩、诚信。生为人，必知礼；亲师恩，铭于心。

· 春生·托天式：顺气疏肝 → 对应"修身·坚韧"——遇逆境，勿轻弃；秉正义，无所惧。

· 夏长·开弓式：静心开胸 → 对应"修身·勇敢"——为人事，信为先；欲成事，切自信。

· 长夏·托举式：化食健脾 → 对应"修身·自信"——求精进，须笃志；宽待人，严律己。

· 秋收·攀足式：清肺润燥 → 对应"守恒·专注"——学所得，践于行。

· 冬藏·颠足式：蓄电补肾 → 对应"守恒·笃行"——莫问所学何以为，但求修身而正德。

练一遍功法，就是践行一遍公武训。

【理论根基·人话版《黄帝内经》】

《黄帝内经》是中国最早的医学经典，两千多年前写的。很多人一听"经"就觉得难。其实它说的事情很简单：

人就是个打气筒。

气进来了，人就有精神。气堵住了，人就生病。气漏光了，人就没了。

所以养生的核心就是三件事：

1. 让气进来（呼吸）

2. 让气流动（运动）

3. 让气留在身体里（不熬夜、不过劳、不做伤身体的事）

我这套功法，就是围绕这三点设计的。

The Yellow Emperor's Inner Canon is China's earliest medical classic, written over two thousand years ago. Many people hear "classic" and think it's difficult. But what it says is very simple:

The human body is an air pump.

When air comes in, you have energy. When air gets blocked, you get sick. When air leaks out completely, life ends.

So the core of health preservation is three things:

1. Let air come in (breathing)

2. Let air flow (movement)

3. Keep air inside the body (don't stay up late, don't overwork, don't do things that harm the body)

This entire practice is designed around these three points.

【功法正篇·五步归元法】

这套功法分五步，对应《黄帝内经》里的五脏（肝心脾肺肾），也对应五季（春夏长夏秋冬）。

下面每一式，我都会写清楚：怎么练、练多久、有什么用、练的时候想什么。

预备式：混元桩（新手从3分钟开始）

对应公武训：立德——知礼、感恩、诚信

怎么练：两脚分开与肩同宽，膝盖微弯像要坐下。双手抱在胸前，像抱一个大西瓜。闭上眼睛，站3分钟。

练多久：早上或晚上站。新手3分钟，慢慢加到15分钟。

有什么用：这是基本功。站一个月，你的腿会变稳，走路不晃，上楼不喘。

心里想什么：想象自己是棵树，脚底下生根，一直长到地底下，谁也推不倒你。正身律己，以武尚德。生为人，必知礼；亲师恩，铭于心。

Preparatory Stance: Primordial Unity Posture (Beginners start with 3 minutes)

Gongwu Code: Establish Virtue — Know Propriety, Be Grateful, Be Sincere

How to practice: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent as if about to sit down. Hold your hands in front of your chest as if embracing a large watermelon. Close your eyes. Stand for 3 minutes.

How long: Practice in the morning or evening. Beginners start with 3 minutes, gradually increase to 15 minutes.

Benefits: This is the foundation. After one month, your legs will become stable, your walk will be steady, and you won't get out of breath going upstairs.

What to visualize: Imagine you are a tree, roots growing from the soles of your feet deep into the earth — so deep that no one can push you over. Rectify the body, discipline the self, honor virtue through martial practice. Born as a person, one must know propriety; honor the kindness of teachers, engrave it upon the heart.

第一式：春生·托天式

对应：肝 | 春季 | 木 | 公武训：修身·坚韧

怎么练：从站桩开始。两手心朝下，慢慢翻上来，顺着肚子前面慢慢举过头顶。抬头看天，深深吸一口气。停住3秒。手心分开，从身体两侧慢慢落下来，呼气。

练几次：做6遍。

有什么用：发脾气、生闷气的人，练这招能"顺气"。对经常熬夜、眼睛干涩的人特别好。

心里想什么：像春天的树苗，破土而出，把郁结在胸口的闷气全顶出去。遇逆境，勿轻弃；秉正义，无所惧。

Movement 1: Spring Growth · Sky-Lifting Form

Corresponds to: Liver | Spring | Wood | Gongwu Code: Cultivate Oneself · Resilience

How to practice: Begin from the standing posture. Palms face down, slowly turn them upward, and raise them along the front of your body until they are above your head. Look up to the sky, take a deep breath. Hold for 3 seconds. Separate your palms and slowly lower them along the sides of your body, exhaling.

Repetitions: 6 times.

Benefits: For those who get angry easily or bottle up their emotions, this movement "smoothes the Qi." Especially good for those who stay up late or have dry eyes.

What to visualize: Like a spring sprout breaking through the soil, pushing all the stagnant air out of your chest. When facing adversity, do not give up lightly; hold fast to justice, and there is nothing to fear.

第二式：夏长·开弓式

对应：心 | 夏季 | 火 | 公武训：修身·勇敢

怎么练：接上一式。双手在胸前平举，左脚向左迈一步，身体下蹲成马步。左手像握弓，右手像拉弦，慢慢拉开。转头看右手。停住3秒。松手，左脚收回，双手落下。

练几次：左右各做6遍。

有什么用：心里烦躁、失眠多梦的人，练这招能"静心"。夏天做特别舒服。

心里想什么：一轮早晨的红太阳照在胸口，暖洋洋的，心里的烦躁全被晒化了。为人事，信为先；欲成事，切自信。

Movement 2: Summer Growth · Bow-Opening Form

Corresponds to: Heart | Summer | Fire | Gongwu Code: Cultivate Oneself · Courage

How to practice: Continue from the previous movement. Raise both hands to chest level, step your left foot out to the side, squat into a horse stance. Your left hand holds the bow; your right hand draws the string, slowly pulling it open. Turn your head to look at your right hand. Hold for 3 seconds. Release, bring your left foot back, and lower your hands.

Repetitions: 6 times on each side.

Benefits: For those who feel agitated, suffer from insomnia, or have restless dreams, this movement "calms the heart." Especially comfortable to do in summer.

What to visualize: A warm morning sun shining on your chest, melting away all the restlessness inside. In dealing with the world, integrity comes first; to accomplish anything, be confident.

第三式：长夏·托举式

对应：脾 | 长夏 | 土 | 公武训：修身·自信

怎么练：回到站桩。右手慢慢往上托，像托一个盘子，一直到头右侧，手心向上。同时左手慢慢往下按，按到大腿旁边，手心向下。身体自然往左边拉伸。停住3秒。慢慢换到另一边。

练几次：左右各做6遍。

有什么用：消化不良、肚子胀气、大便不成形的人，这招是专门"化食"的。饭后一小时做，特别舒服。

心里想什么：像拧毛巾，把肚子里淤积的脏东西，像拧水一样全挤出去。求精进，须笃志；宽待人，严律己。

Movement 3: Late Summer · Lifting and Pressing Form

Corresponds to: Spleen | Late Summer | Earth | Gongwu Code: Cultivate Oneself · Self-Confidence

How to practice: Return to the standing posture. Slowly lift your right hand upward as if holding a tray, all the way to the right side of your head, palm facing up. At the same time, slowly press your left hand downward to the side of your thigh, palm facing down. Let your body naturally stretch to the left. Hold for 3 seconds. Slowly switch to the other side.

Repetitions: 6 times on each side.

Benefits: For those with indigestion, bloating, or loose stools, this movement is specifically designed to "transform food." Especially comfortable to do one hour after meals.

What to visualize: Imagine wringing out a towel — squeezing out all the stagnant stuff accumulated in your belly, like wringing out dirty water. To seek improvement, be resolute in purpose; be generous to others, strict with yourself.

第四式：秋收·攀足式

对应：肺 | 秋季 | 金 | 公武训：守恒·专注

怎么练：站起来，两脚站稳。双手扶着腰，顺着后背慢慢往下推，推到大腿、小腿，一直摸到脚踝。摸不到也没关系，摸到小腿就行。弯腰时慢慢呼气。停住3秒。慢慢站起来。

练几次：做6遍。

有什么用：容易感冒、咳嗽、皮肤干燥的人，这招是"清肺"的。秋天做特别应季。

心里想什么：像秋天的树叶从树上落下来，把肺里的"废料"全呼出去。站起来时，像秋天湖水一样安宁。学所得，践于行。

Movement 4: Autumn Harvest · Reaching the Feet Form

Corresponds to: Lungs | Autumn | Metal | Gongwu Code: Persevere · Focus

How to practice: Stand up, feet firmly planted. Place your hands on your waist, then slowly push down along your back, past your thighs and calves, all the way to your ankles. If you can't reach your ankles, reaching your calves is fine. Exhale slowly as you bend forward. Hold for 3 seconds. Slowly stand back up.

Repetitions: 6 times.

Benefits: For those prone to colds, coughs, or dry skin, this movement "clears the lungs." Especially seasonal to practice in autumn.

What to visualize: Like autumn leaves falling from a tree — breathe out all the "waste" in your lungs. When you rise, feel the calm stillness of an autumn lake. What you learn, put into practice.

第五式：冬藏·颠足式

对应：肾 | 冬季 | 水 | 公武训：守恒·笃行

怎么练：站直，两脚并拢，双手垂在大腿两边。闭上眼睛，全身放松。慢慢踮起脚尖，像头顶有根线把你往上拉。停住3秒。快速落下脚跟——但别用蛮力砸地，让它自然顿一下。你会感觉全身一麻。

练几次：连做7遍。

有什么用：这是整套功法的"发动机"。没精神、腰膝酸软、老得快的人，这招是"充电"的。冬天做就是在给身体"蓄电池"。

心里想什么：想象脚底有一股暖流，从脚后跟钻进来，顺着腿往上走，一直流到小肚子（丹田）。那是你身体里的"电池"，正在蓄电。莫问所学何以为，但求修身而正德。正身律己，以武尚德。

Movement 5: Winter Storage · Heel-Dropping Form

Corresponds to: Kidneys | Winter | Water | Gongwu Code: Persevere · Steadfastness

How to practice: Stand straight, feet together, hands hanging naturally at your sides. Close your eyes, relax your whole body. Slowly rise onto your tiptoes, as if a thread from the top of your head is pulling you upward. Hold for 3 seconds. Quickly drop your heels — but don't slam them down with brute force; let them fall naturally with a slight jolt. You will feel a tingling sensation throughout your body.

Repetitions: 7 times in succession.

Benefits: This is the "engine" of the entire practice. For those lacking energy, suffering from sore knees and lower back, or aging prematurely, this movement "recharges" you. Practicing it in winter is like storing up charge in your body's battery.

What to visualize: Imagine a warm current entering through the soles of your feet, flowing up your legs, and gathering in your lower abdomen (Dantian) — your body's battery, recharging. Do not ask what your learning is for — only seek to cultivate yourself and rectify virtue. Rectify the body, discipline the self, honor virtue through martial practice.

【日常养生·跟太阳学活法】

吃饭：早吃好，午吃饱，晚吃少。小米粥养胃，黑芝麻养头发，白萝卜顺气。山珍海味不如一碗热粥。

睡觉：太阳出来就起，太阳下去就准备睡。晚上11点前上床。别跟老天爷对着干。

心情：有啥不痛快的，找人说说，或者去空旷的地方吼两声。笑一笑，全身经脉都是通的。

Eating: Eat well in the morning, full at noon, and light at night. Millet porridge nourishes the stomach, black sesame nourishes the hair, white radish smooths the Qi. A bowl of hot porridge beats a feast.

Sleeping: Rise with the sun, rest when it sets. Go to bed before 11 PM. Don't fight the natural order.

Mood: If something's bothering you, talk to someone, or go somewhere open and shout it out. A smile opens every meridian in your body.

This set of practices has no fancy names, no complicated moves. It is the most plain, practical health wisdom born in the bones of the Chinese people.

From today, we are the first inheritors of this practice.

If you try it and find it useful, please share it with your family, your friends, your neighbors.

Let this practice spread from one family to a community, from a city to a province, from China to the whole world.

When every ordinary person, whether three or ninety-three years old, can practice their own Chinese health exercises —

we will truly be "carrying on the lost teachings of past sages, and creating lasting peace for all generations."

Rectify the body, discipline the self, honor virtue through martial practice. First establish virtue, then cultivate oneself, and ultimately persevere.

（练功去吧。放下手机，站三分钟桩。你迈出第一步，这套功法就活了一次。）

(Go practice. Put down your phone. Stand in the posture for three minutes. Every time you take that first step, this practice comes alive again.)
