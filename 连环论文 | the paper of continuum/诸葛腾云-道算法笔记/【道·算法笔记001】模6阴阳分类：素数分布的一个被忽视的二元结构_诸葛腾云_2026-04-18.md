**摘要**

本文重新审视一个被基础教育忽略的简单事实：任意大于3的素数，模6余数必为1或5。据此，作者将素数分为“阳素数”（6k+1）与“阴素数”（6k-1）两类，提出“模6阴阳分类”框架。本文论证该分类的自然性与唯一性，指出其在解析数论难题（如哥德巴赫猜想、孪生素数猜想）中对“半素数污染”的识别价值，并简要展望其向神经网络剪枝、信号滤波等应用领域进行思想映射的可能性。本文不涉及核心算法参数与实验数据，仅作原创思想框架的公开宣示。

**一、引言：一个被忽略的素数胎记**

素数理论中，一个几乎无人不知、却鲜有人深究的事实是：除2与3外，任何素数p均可表为p = 6k ± 1（k为正整数）。换言之，模6余数非1即5。这一观察常被当作习题或趣谈，其深层结构意义却被长期低估。

本文旨在确立这一“胎记”的正式框架：模6阴阳分类。作者将余1者称为“阳素数”，余5者称为“阴素数”，并论证此分类为整数在模6下的必然二分，且具有独特的跨领域启发价值。

**二、模6过滤器的必然性**

任意整数n模6，余数必属{0,1,2,3,4,5}。逐一审查：

- n ≡ 0, 2, 4 (mod 6)：n为偶数，除2外必为合数；

- n ≡ 3 (mod 6)：n为3之倍数，除3外必为合数。

故对p>3，欲为素数，仅余p ≡ 1或5 (mod 6)两条路径。此非偶然，而是整数在模6下的自然筛法。因此：

定义（阳素数、阴素数）

- 阳素数：p = 6k+1（如7, 13, 19, 31…）

- 阴素数：p = 6k-1（如5, 11, 17, 23…）

该分类是完备且互斥的，大于3的素数无一例外。

**三、阴阳分类在解析数论中的价值：以“半素数污染”为例**

哥德巴赫猜想、孪生素数猜想等古典难题的现代研究揭示，证明的核心障碍常来自半素数（两素数之积）对真素数结构的“污染”。传统筛法难以有效分离半素数与素数。

在模6阴阳框架下，任一半素数n = p+ × p- 可分解为阳因子与阴因子的乘积。通过构造依赖于二次剩余（勒让德符号）的权函数：

ω_Q(n) = 1/2 [1 - χ(p+(n))]，

可使阴类半素数的统计贡献在特定求和下期望为零——此即“压缩算子”之雏形。更进一步，可在阴类半素数集上构造无不动点的对偶映射，使异号项配对抵消，从而在理论上将污染项边界化。

**四、跨领域映射的可能：从素数到神经网络剪枝**

上述“分类—加权—配对抵消”的思想，不囿于数论。在深度学习模型压缩中，冗余卷积核常被视为“污染项”，但现有剪枝方法依赖启发式阈值，易丢失弱特征。若将卷积核参数离散化并作模6阴阳分类，则有望设计出一种不依赖主观阈值、具有统计抵消特性的结构化剪枝策略。此方向的具体实现与实验验证，因涉及核心参数，本文不作展开。

Modulo-6 Yin-Yang Classification: An Overlooked Binary Structure of Prime Distribution and Its Cross-Domain Implications

Abstract

This paper reexamines a simple yet underappreciated fact: every prime greater than 3 is congruent to either 1 or 5 modulo 6. Based on this, the author partitions primes into Yang primes (6k+1) and Yin primes (6k–1), establishing the "Modulo-6 Yin-Yang Classification" framework. The paper demonstrates the naturalness and uniqueness of this dichotomy, highlights its utility in recognizing "semiprime contamination" within classical problems such as Goldbach's conjecture and the twin prime conjecture, and briefly outlines its potential cross-domain mapping to areas like neural network pruning and signal filtering. Core parameters and experimental data are withheld; this document serves solely as a public declaration of the original conceptual framework.

Keywords: Prime distribution; modulo-6 classification; Yin-Yang; semiprime; compression operator; duality mapping

**1. Introduction: A Neglected Birthmark of Primes**

It is a well-known but seldom scrutinized fact in prime theory that, apart from 2 and 3, every prime p can be expressed as p = 6k ± 1 (k ∈ N+). In other words, modulo 6, the remainder is invariably 1 or 5. This observation is often relegated to exercises or trivia, its deeper structural significance largely overlooked.

This paper aims to formalize this "birthmark" as the Modulo-6 Yin-Yang Classification. The author designates primes congruent to 1 modulo 6 as Yang primes, and those congruent to 5 modulo 6 as Yin primes, arguing that this dichotomy is an inevitable consequence of the modulo-6 sieve and possesses unique cross-domain heuristic value.

**2. The Inevitability of the Modulo-6 Filter**

Any integer n modulo 6 yields a remainder from the set {0,1,2,3,4,5}. A case-by-case examination shows:

- n ≡ 0, 2, 4 (mod 6): n is even, thus composite (except for 2);

- n ≡ 3 (mod 6): n is a multiple of 3, thus composite (except for 3).

Therefore, for p>3 to be prime, the only viable remainders are 1 and 5. This is not happenstance but a natural consequence of the modulo-6 sieve. Hence:

Definition (Yang Primes, Yin Primes)

- Yang Primes: p = 6k+1 (e.g., 7, 13, 19, 31, …)

- Yin Primes: p = 6k-1 (e.g., 5, 11, 17, 23, …)

This classification is exhaustive and mutually exclusive for all primes greater than 3.

**3. Value in Analytic Number Theory: The Case of Semiprime Contamination**

Modern research on classical conjectures like Goldbach's and the twin prime conjecture reveals that a central obstruction stems from semiprimes (products of two primes) contaminating the true prime structure. Traditional sieve methods struggle to separate semiprimes from primes effectively.

Under the modulo-6 Yin-Yang framework, any semiprime n = p+ × p- can be factored into a Yang component and a Yin component. By constructing a weight function that depends on the quadratic residue (Legendre symbol) of the Yang factor:

ω_Q(n) = 1/2 [1 - χ(p+(n))],

the statistical contribution of Yin-type semiprimes can be driven to zero in expectation within specific sums—a prototype of the "compression operator." Furthermore, one may construct a fixed-point-free duality mapping on the set of Yin semiprimes, pairing terms of opposite signs and thereby relegating contamination to a controllable boundary term.

**4. Potential Cross-Domain Mapping: From Primes to Neural Network Pruning**

The "classification–weighting–pairwise cancellation" paradigm is not confined to number theory. In deep learning model compression, redundant convolutional kernels may be regarded as "contaminants," yet existing pruning methods rely on heuristic thresholds and risk discarding weak but essential features. By discretizing kernel parameters and applying the modulo-6 Yin-Yang classification, it becomes conceivable to design a structured pruning strategy that does not depend on subjective thresholds and possesses inherent statistical cancellation properties. Specific implementations and experimental validations are beyond the scope of this conceptual disclosure.
