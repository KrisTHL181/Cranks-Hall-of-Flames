摘要

黎曼猜想断言：黎曼ζ函数的所有非平凡零点都位于临界线Re(s)=1/2上。本文从阴阳分类的视角出发，考察如果存在偏离临界线的零点，素数分布的误差项会呈现何种“阴阳失衡”。我们将揭示，这种失衡会在模6分类的特征和中产生不可抵消的主项，从而为黎曼猜想提供一种新的反证思路。

一、黎曼猜想的阴阳表述

设ψ(x)为切比雪夫函数，素数定理的误差项E(x)=ψ(x)-x。按模6分类，可将素数分为阳素数与阴素数，对应误差项E_+(x)和E_-(x)。如果黎曼猜想成立，则所有零点ρ满足Re(ρ)=1/2，此时E_+(x)与E_-(x)的振荡幅度相同，相位相反，在特征和中相互抵消。

如果存在某个零点ρ_0满足Re(ρ_0)>1/2，则E_+(x)与E_-(x)中会同时出现一个同号的额外主项，其幅度约为x^{Re(ρ_0)}。这个同号项在阴阳特征和中无法通过配对抵消。

二、特征和的不可抵消项

考虑特征和S(x,Q)=∑_{n≤x} χ(n)Λ(n)，其中χ为模Q的二次特征。在黎曼猜想成立时，S(x,Q)=O(x^{1/2+ε})。若存在偏离零点，则S(x,Q)将出现大小为x^{Re(ρ_0)}的主项。通过模6正交性，这一主项在阳素数与阴素数上的投影同号，无法通过对偶映射抵消。这与Burgess界矛盾——因为Burgess界要求任何固定模的特征和必须是o(x)。因此，偏离零点不能存在。

三、阴阳误差的对偶映射

零点本身存在自然对合：ρ → 1-ρ。若Re(ρ)≠1/2，则ρ与1-ρ是两个不同的零点，它们在显式公式中产生的项在阴阳分解后无法抵消。只有当所有零点严格位于临界线上时，ρ与1-ρ互为共轭，阴阳贡献才完美平衡。这便是“零点对偶映射”的思想。

四、框架总结

本文从阴阳分类的视角，为黎曼猜想提供了一条新的反证路径：偏离临界线的零点必然导致特征和中出现不可抵消的主项，与Burgess界矛盾。完整的严格证明需进一步估计特征和的系数，但思想框架已清晰。

Abstract

The Riemann Hypothesis asserts that all non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line Re(s)=1/2. This article examines, from the perspective of yin-yang classification, what kind of "yin-yang imbalance" would appear in the error term of the prime distribution if there existed zeros off the critical line. We reveal that such an imbalance would produce an uncancelable main term in the character sum modulo 6, thereby offering a new reductio ad absurdum approach to the Riemann Hypothesis.

1. A Yin-Yang Formulation of the Riemann Hypothesis

Let ψ(x) be the Chebyshev function, with error term E(x)=ψ(x)-x. Using modulo-6 classification, primes split into Yang and Yin primes, yielding error components E_+(x) and E_-(x). If the Riemann Hypothesis holds, all zeros ρ satisfy Re(ρ)=1/2, and the oscillations of E_+(x) and E_-(x) have equal amplitude but opposite phase, canceling out in character sums.

If there exists a zero ρ_0 with Re(ρ_0)>1/2, then both E_+(x) and E_-(x) acquire an extra main term of the same sign, of magnitude roughly x^{Re(ρ_0)}. This same-sign term cannot be canceled by yin-yang pairing in the character sum.

2. The Uncancelable Term in the Character Sum

Consider the character sum S(x,Q)=∑_{n≤x} χ(n)Λ(n), where χ is a quadratic character modulo Q. Under the Riemann Hypothesis, S(x,Q)=O(x^{1/2+ε}). If an off-critical-line zero exists, S(x,Q) develops a main term of size x^{Re(ρ_0)}. Via modulo-6 orthogonality, this term projects onto Yang and Yin primes with the same sign and cannot be canceled by any duality mapping. This contradicts the Burgess bound, which requires character sums for any fixed modulus to be o(x). Hence, no such zero can exist.

3. Duality Mapping of Zeros

The zeros themselves admit a natural involution: ρ → 1-ρ. If Re(ρ)≠1/2, then ρ and 1-ρ are distinct zeros whose contributions in the explicit formula, after yin-yang decomposition, fail to cancel. Only when all zeros lie exactly on the critical line do ρ and 1-ρ become complex conjugates, achieving perfect yin-yang balance. This is the idea of "zero duality mapping."

4. Framework Summary

This article offers a new reductio pathway for the Riemann Hypothesis via yin-yang classification: any zero off the critical line necessarily produces an uncancelable main term in character sums, contradicting the Burgess bound. A fully rigorous proof requires further estimation of coefficients, but the conceptual framework is clear.
