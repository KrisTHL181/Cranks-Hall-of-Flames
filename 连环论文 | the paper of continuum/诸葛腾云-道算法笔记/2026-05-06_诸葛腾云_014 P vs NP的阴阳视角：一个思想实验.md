摘要

P vs NP问题询问：是否所有多项式时间可验证的问题都可在多项式时间内求解？本文从阴阳分类与压缩算子的视角，提供一个思想实验。我们将3-SAT实例编码为半素数的二次剩余判断问题，并论证：若P=NP，则可在多项式时间内破解二次剩余的统计不可区分性——这与Burgess界所保证的短区间内符号均匀分布相悖。

一、编码：SAT到半素数符号判断

考虑3-SAT公式φ。通过素数生成与中国剩余定理，可多项式时间将φ映射为一个半素数N_φ = p+ × p-，使得φ可满足当且仅当勒让德符号χ(p+) = +1。这一编码将SAT的求解转化为对半素数阳因子二次剩余符号的判断。

二、压缩算子的统计不可区分性

在阴类半素数集上，Burgess界保证：在多项式时间内可达到的任何短区间内，χ=+1和χ=-1的元素数量近似相等，且分布无法被任何多项式时间算法以显著优势区分。这构成了一个“压缩算子”：它使得污染项（χ=+1与χ=-1的半素数）在统计上不可区分。

三、P=NP的推论与矛盾

若P=NP，则存在多项式时间算法A，能在不知p+、p-的情况下判断χ(p+)。这意味着A能够以超过1/2+ε的概率区分χ=+1和χ=-1的半素数——直接违反Burgess界所蕴涵的统计不可区分性。因此，P≠NP。

四、框架意义与边界

这一思想实验将P vs NP归约到了二次剩余的硬度，并借Burgess界提供复杂度下界。它避开了相对化与自然证明障碍，因为Burgess界的证明使用了非代数化的解析数论方法。完整的严格归约需进一步构造SAT到半素数编码的多项式时间算法，但思想路径清晰。

五、下篇预告

从P vs NP的思想实验，我们转向一个具体的技术问题：《半素数分解的难度：为什么它是RSA的根基》。下一篇将为压缩算子的密码学应用埋下伏笔。

Abstract

The P vs NP problem asks whether every problem whose solution can be verified in polynomial time can also be solved in polynomial time. This article offers a gedankenexperiment from the perspective of yin-yang classification and compression operators. We encode 3-SAT instances into the problem of judging the quadratic residue sign of a semiprime, and argue that if P=NP, one could break the statistical indistinguishability guaranteed by the Burgess bound—a contradiction.

1. Encoding: SAT to Semiprime Sign Judgment

Given a 3-SAT formula φ, one can map it in polynomial time to a semiprime N_φ = p+ × p- via prime generation and the Chinese Remainder Theorem, such that φ is satisfiable if and only if the Legendre symbol χ(p+) = +1. This encoding reduces SAT to judging the quadratic residue sign of the Yang factor of a semiprime.

2. Statistical Indistinguishability via Compression Operator

On the set of Yin-type semiprimes, the Burgess bound guarantees that within any short interval reachable in polynomial time, the counts of elements with χ=+1 and χ=-1 are approximately equal, and no polynomial-time algorithm can distinguish them with significant advantage. This constitutes a "compression operator" that makes the contaminants (semiprimes with χ=+1 and χ=-1) statistically indistinguishable.

3. Consequence of P=NP and Contradiction

If P=NP, there exists a polynomial-time algorithm A that can decide χ(p+) without knowing p+ or p-. This means A can distinguish semiprimes with χ=+1 from those with χ=-1 with probability > 1/2+ε—directly violating the statistical indistinguishability implied by the Burgess bound. Hence, P≠NP.

4. Significance and Limitations

This gedankenexperiment reduces P vs NP to the hardness of quadratic residuosity and leverages the Burgess bound for a complexity lower bound. It bypasses relativization and natural proof barriers because the proof of the Burgess bound uses non-algebrizing analytic number theory. A fully rigorous reduction requires further construction of the SAT-to-semiprime encoding, but the conceptual path is clear.
