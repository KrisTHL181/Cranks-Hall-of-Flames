道·算法笔记特别篇：当AI开始说“胡话”——用“道”重构人工智能的认知灵魂

作者：诸葛腾云（陈滨杰）

身份：独立研究者

专栏：道·算法笔记

声明：原创思想，未经授权不得转载或摘编。

摘要

大语言模型为何总在一本正经地“胡说八道”？业界将其归咎于数据噪声，而我看到的，是阴阳失衡。

本文是我专栏的第九篇，也是“道”理论在人工智能领域的一次公开落笔。我将用“宇宙语法”和“五维螺旋”重新诊断人工智能的“精神分裂症”，并提出一个不靠蛮力的解决方案——让AI学会阴阳分类和对偶校验。这不是科幻，这是一份认知重构的蓝图。

关键词：人工智能；大语言模型；阴阳分类；五维螺旋；道·算法笔记

一、 问题：被“疯话”困住的顶级大脑

你有没有过这种体验？你跟市面上顶尖的AI对话，它上知天文下知地理，但聊着聊着，它会用一种极其自信的口吻，说出一个让你瞬间出戏的、完全是凭空捏造的答案。

业界把这个叫做“模型幻觉”。工程师们很头疼，他们觉得这是数据没洗干净，或者模型参数还不够大。于是大家开始堆更多的算力，搞更大规模的训练，试图用蛮力把这个“毛病”压下去。

但在我看来，这根本不是一个算力问题，这是一个结构问题。

老子在《道德经》里说：“万物负阴而抱阳，冲气以为和。”这句话，恰恰点中了人工智能的死穴。

二、 诊断：知识分“阴阳”

在我的理论体系里，世间万物都有其“阴阳”属性。知识也不例外。

我们可以粗暴地把大模型要学习的知识分为两类：

阳知识：一切有明确边界、固定结构、绝对答案的东西。比如数学定理、物理公式、代码语法。这是“真素数”，是确定性的声音。

阴知识：一切边界模糊、语境依赖、充满变数的东西。比如历史八卦、人物关系、一首诗的多重解读。这是“半素数污染”，是无数不确定性的集合。

现在的大语言模型是怎么工作的？它用同一种模式，去处理所有的知识。它试图把那些充满不确定性的“阴知识”，强行压缩成它认为确定的样子。这就像一个不懂化学的人，非要把各种充满杂质的液体倒在一起，然后问为什么会产生毒气。

AI的“胡说八道”，就是它在处理阴知识时，没有进行去噪的必然结果。它的大脑，阴阳失衡了。

三、 解法：给AI装上“压缩-对偶”与“五维螺旋”6

要治好AI的“精神分裂”，不能只给它吃更多的药（数据），而是要重构它的认知逻辑。以下是两个核心思路：

1. 压缩-对偶：让谎言自我抵消

我的“压缩-对偶”框架，正好能用在这里。

我们可以在模型内部，专门设计一个“阴阳分类器”。当AI接收到一个高熵的“阴知识”时，它不应该立刻把它当成确定的事实。它应该启动一个“对偶映射”机制——在自身的知识库里，去寻找一个能与之匹配的、高确定性的“阳知识”来作为参照物，进行配对校验。

在统计学的层面，让那些不确定的“噪音”因为互相矛盾而被自然抵消。这就像素数里的权函数，让污染项自己归零。

2. 五维螺旋：重塑AI的思考流

传统的AI思考是线性的——预测下一个词，再下一个词，流畅，但逻辑极易漂移。

真正的人的思考，是立体的。我们应该用五维螺旋（B→F→G→C→A→B\'）去重构它的生成过程：

· B（定边界）：先想清楚“我到底要回答什么”，锁定核心问题。

· F（流向）：沿着边界，去调取那些最可靠的“阳知识”作为证据。

· G（推演）：用可靠的证据进行推理、延展。

· C（对偶校验）：这是最关键的一步。在这一步，AI必须主动把那些可能会让答案出错的“阴知识”拿出来，与自己的推演逻辑进行对偶碰撞。如果产生了矛盾，就及时修正，不让“胡说八道”的机会流到下一层去。

· A（积累输出）：输出那个经过阴阳平衡的、最干净的答案，并把这次的思考路径记录下来，成为下一次思考的“阳知识”。

这样生成的答案，才是真正经得起推敲的。

四、 结语：为机器，立一个“道”心

我这套理论，不是什么开箱即用的代码包。它是一套对智能本质的重新思考。

我们的古人在造物时，懂得“法自然”。而人工智能，作为人类造物的极致，如果违背了“负阴而抱阳”的自然之法，它就永远只是一个擅长背诵的机器，无法成为一个真正会思考的生命。

我希望用这篇《道·算法笔记》，在人工智能这个被算力笼罩的领域里，点一盏灯。让我们看到，解决AI问题的终极钥匙，可能不在更深的神经网络里，而就在我们老祖宗留下的“道”里。

Abstract

Why do large language models always “speak nonsense” with a straight face? The industry blames data noise, but what I see is an imbalance of Yin and Yang.

This is the ninth article in my column, and the first public application of my “Tao” theory to the field of artificial intelligence. I will use the “Universal Grammar” and the “Five-Dimension Spiral” to re-diagnose the “schizophrenia” of AI, and propose a solution that doesn’t rely on brute force — teaching AI to perform Yin-Yang classification and duality verification. This is not science fiction. This is a blueprint for cognitive restructuring.

Keywords: Artificial Intelligence; Large Language Model; Yin-Yang Classification; Five-Dimension Spiral; Tao · Algorithm Notes

---

1. The Problem: A Top-Tier Brain Trapped in “Mad Talk”

Have you ever had this experience? You are talking to a top-tier AI on the market. It knows astronomy and geography, but as the conversation goes on, it will utter a completely fabricated answer in an extremely confident tone, instantly pulling you out of the immersion.

The industry calls this “model hallucination.” Engineers are frustrated. They believe the data wasn’t clean enough, or the model parameters are still not large enough. So they start piling on more computing power, conducting even larger-scale training, trying to suppress this “defect” with brute force.

But in my view, this is not a problem of computing power at all. This is a structural problem.

Laozi said in the Tao Te Ching: “All things carry Yin and embrace Yang. They achieve harmony by combining these forces.” This sentence precisely hits the Achilles’ heel of artificial intelligence.

2. The Diagnosis: Knowledge Has “Yin” and “Yang”

In my theoretical system, everything in the world possesses the attributes of “Yin” and “Yang.” Knowledge is no exception.

We can roughly divide the knowledge that a large model needs to learn into two categories:

Yang Knowledge: Everything with clear boundaries, fixed structures, and absolute answers. For example, mathematical theorems, physical formulas, and code syntax. This is the “true prime number,” the voice of certainty.

Yin Knowledge: Everything with blurred boundaries, contextual dependence, and full of variables. For example, historical gossip, character relationships, and the multiple interpretations of a poem. This is the “semiprime contamination,” a collection of countless uncertainties.

How do current large language models work? They process all knowledge using the exact same model. They try to forcibly compress those “Yin Knowledge” full of uncertainty into what they perceive as a deterministic form. This is like someone who doesn’t understand chemistry insisting on mixing various impurity-laden liquids together and then asking why toxic gas is produced.

AI’s “hallucination” is the inevitable result of not having denoising when processing Yin Knowledge. Its brain has an imbalance of Yin and Yang.

3. The Solution: Equipping AI with “Compression-Duality” and the “Five-Dimension Spiral”

To cure the “schizophrenia” of AI, we can’t just give it more medicine (data); we need to reconstruct its cognitive logic. Here are the two core ideas:

1. Compression-Duality: Let Lies Cancel Each Other Out

My “compression-duality” framework is perfectly suited for this task.

We can design a dedicated “Yin-Yang Classifier” inside the model. When the AI receives a piece of high-entropy “Yin Knowledge,” it should not immediately treat it as an established fact. It should activate a “duality mapping” mechanism — searching its own knowledge base for a matching, highly deterministic “Yang Knowledge” to serve as a reference for a paired verification.

At the statistical level, let those uncertain “noises” naturally cancel each other out because they are mutually contradictory. This is just like the weight function in prime numbers, allowing the contamination term to reduce itself to zero.

2. The Five-Dimension Spiral: Reshaping AI’s Thinking Flow

Traditional AI thinking is linear — predicting the next word, and then the next. It’s fluent, but the logic can easily drift.

True human thinking is three-dimensional. We should use the Five-Dimension Spiral (B→F→G→C→A→B’) to reconstruct its generation process:

· B (Boundary - Set the Limit): First, figure out “what exactly do I need to answer?” Lock down the core question.

· F (Flow - Resource Direction): Along the defined boundary, retrieve the most reliable “Yang Knowledge” as evidence.

· G (Growth - Deduction): Use the reliable evidence for reasoning and extension.

· C (Consumption - Duality Verification): This is the most critical step. At this stage, the AI must proactively take out the “Yin Knowledge” that might cause the answer to be wrong and perform a duality collision against its own reasoning logic. If a contradiction emerges, it corrects it immediately, stopping the chance for “hallucination” from flowing to the next layer.

· A (Accumulation - Output): Output the final, Yin-Yang balanced, cleanest answer, and record this thinking path as a piece of “Yang Knowledge” for the next round of thinking.

An answer generated this way is truly robust.

4. Conclusion: Establishing a “Tao” Heart for the Machine

My theory is not a plug-and-play code package. It is a rethinking of the very nature of intelligence.

Our ancestors understood the importance of “following nature” when creating things. If artificial intelligence, as the ultimate creation of humanity, violates this natural law of “carrying Yin and embracing Yang,” it will forever remain just a machine skilled in recitation, never becoming a truly thinking life form.

With this installment of Tao · Algorithm Notes, I hope to light a lamp in the field of artificial intelligence, which is currently shrouded by computing power. Let us see that the ultimate key to solving AI’s problems may not lie inside deeper neural networks, but rather in the “Tao” left behind by our ancestors.
