摘要

压缩算子将污染项分成了χ=+1和χ=-1两半。但要真正让它们在总和中抵消，需要一个配对机制。本文引入“对偶映射”——一种无不动点的符号翻转对合。我们将证明，在短区间内，这种配对总是存在，且位移可控。这是整个框架的“抵消引擎”。

一、问题的提出

权函数ω=½(1-χ)让χ=+1的阴类半6素数权重归零，χ=-1的保留权重1。但在实际求和中（如哥德巴赫分拆的加权计数），我们无法单独挑出χ=-1的项——我们只能对集合中的所有元素求和，然后期望χ=+1和χ=-1的贡献相互抵消。

这需要一个配对机制：将每个χ=+1的项，与一个χ=-1的项配对，让它们在求和中符号相反，贡献抵消。这个配对，必须是一个定义在阴类半素数集合上的映射σ，满足两个条件：

1. 无不动点：σ(n)≠n，每个元素都被移走。

2. 符号翻转：χ(p+(σ(n))) = -χ(p+(n))。

二、对偶映射的严格定义

设S为阴类半素数集合。映射σ:S→S称为一个对偶映射，如果：

· σ是双射（一一对应）；

· σ(σ(n))=n（对合性，两次映射回到自身）；

· σ(n)≠n（无不动点）；

· χ(p+(σ(n))) = -χ(p+(n))（符号翻转）。

这样的σ将S中的元素配成了若干对(n, σ(n))，每对内部符号相反。在求和中，每一对的贡献之和的期望为零。

三、存在性：短区间内的贪心配对

这样的σ是否存在？答案是肯定的，前提是我们允许一定的“位移”。具体地，存在一个常数θ<1（基于Burgess界，可取θ≈7/12），使得对于任意n∈S，我们总能在区间[n, n+O(n^θ)]内找到一个m∈S，满足χ(p+(m)) = -χ(p+(n))。

存在性的证明依赖Burgess关于短区间内特征和的上界估计。Burgess界保证了：在长度为n^θ的短区间内，χ=+1和χ=-1的阴类半素数数量近似相等，且都足够多。因此，贪心算法可以成功配对绝大多数元素，未配对的边界项数量远小于主项。

四、位移界与边界项

配对不是免费的。位移|n-σ(n)|的大小，决定了我们在求和时引入的“边界项”大小。由Burgess界，位移可控制在O(n^θ)。在总和中，这些边界项的贡献被证明是低阶小量，不影响主项的下界。

五、下篇预告

有了权函数和对偶映射，压缩-对偶框架的数学骨架已完整。下一篇，我们将回到具体问题：在哥德巴赫猜想的加权分拆中，这套框架如何让半素数污染项“消失”。敬请期待《特征和的抵消：如何用配对让误差项“统计归零”》。

Abstract

The compression operator splits contaminants into two halves: χ=+1 and χ=-1. But to truly cancel them out in a sum, a pairing mechanism is required. This article introduces the "duality mapping"—a fixed-point-free sign-flipping involution. We will show that such pairings always exist within short intervals, with controllable displacement. This is the "cancellation engine" of the entire framework.

1. Statement of the Problem

The weight function ω=½(1-χ) zeroes out the weight of Yin-type semiprimes with χ=+1 and retains weight 1 for those with χ=-1. However, in actual summations (such as the weighted count of Goldbach partitions), we cannot isolate terms with χ=-1—we can only sum over all elements in the set and hope that the contributions of χ=+1 and χ=-1 cancel each other out.

This requires a pairing mechanism: pair each term with χ=+1 to a term with χ=-1, so that their signs oppose and their contributions cancel in the sum. This pairing must be a map σ defined on the set of Yin-type semiprimes, satisfying two conditions:

1. Fixed-point-free: σ(n) ≠ n, every element is moved.

2. Sign-flipping: χ(p+(σ(n))) = -χ(p+(n)).

2. Rigorous Definition of Duality Mapping

Let S be the set of Yin-type semiprimes. A map σ: S → S is called a duality mapping if:

· σ is a bijection (one-to-one correspondence);

· σ(σ(n)) = n (involution: applying it twice returns to the original element);

· σ(n) ≠ n (no fixed points);

· χ(p+(σ(n))) = -χ(p+(n)) (sign-flipping).

Such a σ partitions S into pairs (n, σ(n)), with opposite signs within each pair. In a sum, the expected contribution of each pair is zero.

3. Existence: Greedy Pairing Within Short Intervals

Does such a σ exist? The answer is yes, provided we allow some "displacement." Specifically, there exists a constant θ < 1 (based on the Burgess bound, one may take θ ≈ 7/12) such that for any n ∈ S, we can always find an m ∈ S within the interval [n, n + O(n^θ)] satisfying χ(p+(m)) = -χ(p+(n)).

The proof of existence relies on Burgess's upper bound for character sums over short intervals. The Burgess bound guarantees that within a short interval of length n^θ, the counts of Yin-type semiprimes with χ=+1 and χ=-1 are approximately equal and sufficiently numerous. Hence, a greedy algorithm can successfully pair the vast majority of elements, leaving only a boundary set of unpaired terms whose size is much smaller than the main term.

4. Displacement Bound and Boundary Terms

Pairing is not free. The magnitude of displacement |n - σ(n)| determines the size of the "boundary terms" introduced into the summation. By the Burgess bound, the displacement can be controlled to O(n^θ). In the overall sum, the contribution of these boundary terms is shown to be a lower-order quantity that does not affect the lower bound of the main term.

5. Preview of the Next Article

With the weight function and duality mapping in place, the mathematical skeleton of the compression-duality framework is complete.
