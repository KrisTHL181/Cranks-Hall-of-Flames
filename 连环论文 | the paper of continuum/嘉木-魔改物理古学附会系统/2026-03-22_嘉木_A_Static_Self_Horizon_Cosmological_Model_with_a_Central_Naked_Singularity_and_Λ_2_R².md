$ds^2=-A(r)c^2 dt^2+B(r) dr^2+r^2\left(d\theta^2+\sin^2\theta d\phi^2\right)$ .

Abstract

We present an exact static, spherically symmetric cosmological solution to the Einstein field equations with a central naked singularity and a cosmological constant $\Lambda=2/R^2$ . The model is uniquely determined by the self-horizon condition $r=2GM(r)/c^2$ and the gravitational redshift relation 1+z=R/r. The spacetime has constant scalar curvature and is regular everywhere except at the central singularity. Without invoking cosmic expansion or the Big Bang, the model rigorously combines general relativistic geometry and special relativistic kinematics to naturally explain the nonlinear Hubble flow and the physical origin of the Hubble tension. This work aims to provide a new pathway for explaining the real universe within a self-consistent and observationally testable general relativistic framework.

Keywords: general relativity; naked singularity; static universe; self-horizon; cosmological constant; gravitational redshift; exact solution; Hubble tension; relativistic cosmology

1. Introduction

The standard FLRW cosmological model provides a well‑established description of cosmic expansion, the cosmic microwave background, and large‑scale structure. However, several fundamental open problems remain, including the nature of the cosmological singularity, the fine‑tuning of the cosmological constant, the physical origin of cosmic redshift, and the Hubble tension.

As an alternative mathematically consistent framework, we construct a static, spherically symmetric cosmological model with a central naked singularity, based on the self-horizon condition, gravitational redshift, and $\Lambda=2/R^2$ . The model is derived rigorously within general relativity without assuming spacetime expansion.

The purpose of this work is to provide a new pathway for explaining the real universe by investigating whether a static, self‑horizon geometry can offer a physically reasonable and theoretically complete alternative to standard cosmology.

2. Geometric Setup and Theoretical Constraints

We consider a static, spherically symmetric spacetime in Schwarzschild‑like coordinates ( $t, r, \theta, \phi$ ). All physical and geometric quantities are time‑independent.

- Central singularity: A physical curvature singularity exists at r=0, which is naked and observable.

- Observer location: The observer is located at r=R, where R is the characteristic cosmic scale.

- Self-horizon condition: For any radial coordinate r,

$r=\frac{2GM(r)}{c^2}$ .

- Gravitational redshift: Cosmic redshift arises from the static spacetime geometry:

$1+z=\frac{R}{r}$ .

- Cosmological constant: Uniquely determined by the cosmic scale:

$\Lambda=\frac{2}{R^2}$ .

These constraints form a closed system and uniquely determine the entire spacetime structure.

The general static spherically symmetric line element is

$ds^2=-A(r)c^2 dt^2+B(r) dr^2+r^2\left(d\theta^2+\sin^2\theta d\phi^2\right)$ .

From the gravitational redshift condition:

$1+z=\sqrt{\frac{A(R)}{A(r)}}=\frac{R}{r}$ .

Normalizing at the observer position A(R)=1, we obtain

$A(r)=\frac{r^2}{R^2}$ .

Using the self-horizon condition and $\Lambda=2/R^2$ , the radial function is uniquely determined:

$B(r)=\frac{3R^2}{2r^2}$ .

The exact line element is therefore

$\boxed{ ds^2=-\frac{r^2}{R^2}c^2 dt^2+\frac{3R^2}{2r^2} dr^2+r^2\left(d\theta^2+\sin^2\theta d\phi^2\right) }.$

5.1 Relativistic Distance, Recession Velocity, and the Origin of the Hubble Tension

$\boxed{d = \frac{Rz}{1+z}}$ .

The corresponding relativistic recession velocity is

$\boxed{v = c\frac{z^2 + 2z}{z^2 + 2z + 2}}$ .

Using the definition H(z)=v/d, we obtain

$\boxed{H(z) = \frac{c}{R}\,k(z)}$ ,

where

$k(z) = \frac{(1+z)\sqrt{1-\beta^2}}{\beta},\quad \beta = \frac{v}{c}$ .

The factor k(z) is a redshift-dependent relativistic correction from combined special and general relativistic spacetime transformations, and represents the physical origin of the Hubble tension. The conventional linear approximation neglects k(z), leading to inconsistent measurements of $H_0$ at different redshifts. The Hubble tension is therefore not a theoretical crisis, but an apparent effect caused by incomplete relativistic modeling.

7. Conclusions

We have presented a new exact static cosmological solution within general relativity, featuring a central naked singularity, constant scalar curvature, and global self‑horizon geometry. The model is fully self-consistent, mathematically well‑defined, and free of internal contradictions.

In contrast to the standard expanding paradigm, this framework interprets cosmic redshift as a gravitational effect rather than spatial expansion, and the cosmological constant as a geometric consequence of the cosmic scale rather than an arbitrary free parameter. The model further incorporates special relativistic kinematics to provide an exact nonlinear description of the Hubble flow, identifying k(z) as the relativistic correction responsible for the Hubble tension.

This work provides a new pathway for explaining the real universe and demonstrates that a consistent, non‑expanding, geometrically closed cosmic structure is theoretically viable within Einstein’s general relativity, while successfully explaining cosmic redshift, the CMB, the cosmological constant, and the Hubble tension.
