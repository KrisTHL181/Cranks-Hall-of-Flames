关键词：时间本质（Nature of Time） 时空质量密度 （Spacetime Mass Density） 宇宙红移 （Cosmological Redshift）

时间是度量空间长度的刻度单位，由时空质量密度来定义。

换句话来说时间就是在光速不变的条件下，体现为频率与观察者所在时空质量密度的对应关系。光速不变，当观察者所在时空质量密度增大时，光频率增高，时间变慢；当观察者所在时空质量密度变小时，光频率降低，时间变快。 多普勒效应 和宇宙红移现象和所谓的 狭义相对论 原理都是观察者所在时空质量密度变化产生的结果。

因此时间本质上是观察者所在时空空间长度单位刻度；代表对相同空间间隔的划分。

t=-r/c

这是对绝对时空的时间定义，t≤0，代表远离宇宙中心方向为已经存在的过去。其中：

r——以宇宙中心奇点为极坐标原点的极坐标轴

c——光速，为299792458m/s

相对于观察者所在时空，有

r=R-D

恒成立；其中：

D——被观察者距观察者的视向距离

R—— 宇宙视界半径 ，R=cT

r——被观察者距宇宙中心的距离,r=-ct

意味着在宇宙时空内时间的未来的方向的顺序是观察者，被观察者，宇宙中心，三者永远在同一条光线上；分别为0时刻，未来-t时刻，未来T时刻。

如果光线传播方向是从宇宙中心、被观察者至观察者，则代表着0时刻，过去T+t时刻，过去T时刻。

r=R-D

称为宇宙 广义相对论 时空坐标变换公式

其中D与宇宙视界半径R之间存在如下函数关系：

\[

D = R \left(1 - e^{-Z}\right)

\]

![](https://pic2.zhimg.com/v2-27159e1f16329b0e67c12971303929e9_1440w.jpg)

其中

Z——本超星系范围外的被观察者所在时空位置发出的光线传播至观察者所在时空过程中产生的红移值

该式称为 宇宙学红移定律 公式， 哈勃定律 公式是该公式的一阶泰勒近似。

由此可见，宇宙学红移与狭义相对论原理一样是宇宙时空特性，是广义相对论时空特性的具体体现。与所谓的“暗能量”或“宇宙加速膨胀”无关。

**Time** is a unit scale for measuring spatial length, defined by the mass density of spacetime.

In other words, time is the correspondence between frequency and the mass density of the observer's spacetime, under the condition of a constant speed of light. With the speed of light constant, when the mass density of the observer's spacetime increases, the light frequency increases and time slows down; when the mass density decreases, the light frequency decreases and time speeds up. The Doppler effect, cosmological redshift phenomena, and the so-called principle of special relativity are all results of changes in the mass density of the observer's spacetime.

Therefore, time is essentially the unit scale of spatial length in the observer's spacetime; it represents the division of the same spatial interval.

`t = -r / c`

This defines time in absolute spacetime. `t ≤ 0`, meaning the direction away from the cosmic center points to the existing past. Where:

*   `r` — The polar coordinate axis with the cosmic central singularity as the origin.

*   `c` — The speed of light, equal to 299,792,458 m/s.

Relative to the observer's spacetime, the following relation always holds:

`r = R - D`

Where:

*   `D` — The line-of-sight distance from the observer to the observed.

*   `R` — The cosmic horizon radius, `R = cT` (where `T` is the age of the universe).

*   `r` — The distance of the observed from the cosmic center, `r = -ct`.

This means that within cosmic spacetime, the temporal sequence towards the future is: Observer → Observed → Cosmic Center. These three always lie on the same light ray, corresponding to moment `0`, future moment `-t`, and future moment `T`, respectively.

If the light propagation direction is from the Cosmic Center → Observed → Observer, it corresponds to moment `0`, past moment `T+t`, and past moment `T`, respectively.

`r = R - D`

is called the **Cosmic General Relativistic Spacetime Coordinate Transformation Formula**.

The line-of-sight distance `D` and the cosmic horizon radius `R` have the following functional relationship:

`D = R (1 - e^{-Z})`

Where:

*   `Z` — The **redshift value** produced during the propagation of light emitted from the spacetime position of the observed **outside the Local Supercluster** to the observer's spacetime.

This formula is called the **Cosmological Redshift Law Formula**. The Hubble's Law formula is its first-order Taylor approximation.

It can thus be seen that cosmological redshift, like the principle of special relativity, is a fundamental characteristic of cosmic spacetime and a specific manifestation of the spacetime properties described by General Relativity. It is unrelated to so-called "dark energy" or "cosmic accelerated expansion".
