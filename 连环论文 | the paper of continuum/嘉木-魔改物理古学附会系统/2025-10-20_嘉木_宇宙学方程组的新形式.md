①②③为 宇宙学方程组 的新形式，是宇宙时空特性的宏观表述，描述的是观察者与被观察者之间时空变换关系：

d=R-r     ——①

Z=ln(R/r)——②

t=-r/c     ——③

其中

d——观察者距其所在“本”超星系范围外的被观察者之间的视向距离（“本”超星系范围定义为观察者所在的引力势阱所形成的 引力透镜 体内，即观察者可观测到的蓝移天体范围内”）

Z—— 广义相对论 时空变换因子，值为光线在观察者和被观察者之间传播过程中产生的 红移值

R——宇宙时空空间常量，值为 宇宙视界半径 ，等于观察者距宇宙中心/视界的视向距离

r——被观察者距宇宙中心/视界的视向距离，取值范围0-R

t——宇宙时空的单位刻度，方向指向观察者，t≤0（取值范围0-R/T，T为宇宙时空的时间跨度），代表着时间方向指向已经存在的过去；换个视角，观察者视向代表着未来，时间方向为正。

宇宙学方程组新形式表明，宇宙具有恒态的时空结构，所有的被观察者都来自过去，正在奔向未来。任一时空位置上的观察者等效且各向同性，没有居于特殊位置的观察者。

> Title: The Cosmological Horizon as a Naked Singularity: A New Framework Based on Spacetime Topology and the Future-Time Arrow 
>  Abstract 
>  Context.The standard ΛCDM model, while successful, leaves foundational questions about the nature of redshift, the cosmic horizon, and the arrow of time open for discussion. 
>  Aims.We propose a novel cosmological framework that reinterprets key observational phenomena within a static spacetime geometry, positing a central naked singularity expressed as the cosmological horizon. 
>  Methods.Starting from the fundamental postulate of a finite cosmic horizon  R  as a universal scale, we derive three core equations describing the relationship between an observer and any observed object. These equations are shown to embody three physical principles: a spatial topological principle, a future-time arrow principle, and an optical principle. 
>  Results.The model naturally yields a cosmological redshift,  Z = \ln(R/r) , interpreted as accumulated gravitational redshift within this static geometry. The theory inherently satisfies the cosmological principle, with all comoving observers at  r = R  being equivalent. The revised lookback time  \tau = r/c  provides a clear and consistent geometric origin for the arrow of time. The framework offers qualitative agreement with the redshift-distance relation of ΛCDM at intermediate redshifts. 
>  Conclusions.This work presents a coherent paradigm where the horizon is not an emergent property of expansion but the optical manifestation of a central naked singularity in our causal past. It provides a geometric basis for the arrow of time and challenges conventional views on cosmic evolution. Future work will focus on deriving the metric from first principles and seeking definitive observational tests.

>  1 Introduction 
>  The prevailing ΛCDM cosmological model provides a remarkably successful description of the universe's evolution from a hot, dense initial state. Its pillars, such as the cosmic microwave background and the redshift-distance relation, are strongly supported by observational data. Despite its successes, the model incorporates profound conceptual puzzles, including the nature of the initial singularity, the origin of the arrow of time, and the cause of the observed cosmological redshift, often attributed to the expansion of space itself. 
>  This paper explores an alternative interpretation. We propose a framework that shifts the focus from a dynamically evolving universe to a static spacetime geometry whose observable properties are determined by the fundamental relationship between any observer and a causal boundary—the cosmological horizon. We posit that this horizon is not a feature of an expanding universe but the optical signature of a naked singularity that topologically defines the cosmos. 
>  In this model, the redshift of distant galaxies, the existence of a horizon, and the one-directional flow of time are all emergent properties of this underlying static geometry. Section 2 outlines the theoretical framework and derives the core equations from three fundamental principles. Section 3 compares the model's predictions with standard observations and discusses its implications, particularly its reconciliation with the cosmological principle and its novel explanation for the arrow of time. Our conclusions are presented in Section 4. 
>  2 Theoretical Framework 
>  2.1 Definitions and Postulates 
>  The model is built upon the following definitions and foundational postulates: 
>  1. Cosmic Horizon ( R ): A universal spatial constant, representing the radius of the cosmological horizon. It is the maximum possible line-of-sight distance for any comoving observer and is identified as the spatial representation of a central naked singularity. 
>  2. Radial Coordinate ( r ): The radial distance of a celestial object from the cosmic horizon/singularity. Its value ranges from  0  (at the horizon) to  R  (at the observer's location). 
>  3. Local Gravitational Domain: The region surrounding an observer where local gravitational potentials are sufficient to produce observable blueshifts. This domain corresponds approximately to the condition  r \to R . 
>  4. The Cosmological Principle: The model is constructed to satisfy this principle. Any comoving observer positioned at  r = R  will measure the same value of  R  and observe the same universal laws, with an isotropic sky. 
>  2.2 The Core Cosmological Equations 
>  The framework is defined by three equations relating the observer at  r = R  to a source at coordinate  r : 
>  1. The Line-of-Sight Distance Equation: 
>  \begin{equation} 
>  d = R - r 
>  \end{equation} 
>  This defines the proper spatial distance  d  between the observer and the source, encapsulating the topology of a space originating from the horizon. 
>  2. The Cosmological Redshift Equation: 
>  \begin{equation} 
>  Z = \ln(R / r) 
>  \end{equation} 
>  This gives the redshift of light emitted from the source, interpreted not as a Doppler shift but as an accumulated gravitational redshift due to the spacetime curvature from the central singularity. 
>  3. The Lookback Time Equation: 
>  \begin{equation} 
>  \tau = r / c 
>  \end{equation} 
>  This defines the lookback time  \tau —the time required for light to travel from the source to the observer. It is a positive, measurable quantity that points unequivocally into the observer's past. 
>  2.3 Physical Core: The Cosmic Horizon as a Naked Singularity 
>  The model's foundation is the interpretation of the global spacetime structure as originating from a naked singularity. This singularity is not hidden within a black hole but is exposed to all observers as their collective cosmological horizon. This interpretation is articulated through three physical principles: 
>  1. The Spatial Topological Principle: 
>  The universe's global spatial structure is topologically defined by a central singularity, which manifests as the cosmological horizon at  r=0 . The coordinate  r  measures inward from this horizon. The geometry described by Eq. (1) is static and compact, with all galactic recession phenomena being a geometric consequence of sources being located at different depths within this topology relative to the singularity. 
>  2. The Future-Time Arrow Principle: 
>  General relativity does not inherently explain the arrow of time. This model provides a geometric origin: the irreversible future-time arrow arises because all observers reside within the future light cone of the central singularity. The lookback time in Eq. (3) signifies the time elapsed since a causal signal from the singularity/horizon ( r=0, \tau=0 ) reached a point at coordinate  r . Observers can only receive information from their past ( \tau > 0 ), establishing a fundamental, geometrically defined direction for time, aligned with the thermodynamic arrow. 
>  3. The Optical Principle: 
>  The central naked singularity is not seen directly but optically transforms itself into the cosmological horizon through extreme gravitational lensing. Equation (2),  Z = \ln(R/r) , is precisely the accumulated gravitational redshift. A photon travelling from a source at  r  to the observer at  R  effectively climbs out of the immense gravitational potential well associated with the singularity. Sources closer to the horizon (smaller  r ) experience a greater redshift, with  Z \to \infty  as  r \to 0 , consistent with the impossibility of receiving signals from the horizon itself. 
>  3 Comparison with Observations and Discussion 
>  3.1 Redshift-Distance Relation 
>  Combining Eqs. (1) and (2) yields the implicit redshift-distance relation within this framework. By selecting a value for  R  (e.g., of the order of 14 Gpc), the model's prediction qualitatively matches the redshift-distance relation of the ΛCDM model for redshifts  Z < 1 . This demonstrates that the observed recession of galaxies can be reproduced within a static spacetime geometry. 
>  However, for high-redshift objects ( Z > 1.5 ), the predictions of the two models diverge. The interpretation of the cosmic microwave background (CMB) at  Z \approx 1100  within this model would require additional physics or different initial conditions at the horizon boundary, presenting a key area for future testing. 
>  3.2 Resolution of Conceptual Issues 
>  · The Arrow of Time: The revised lookback time  \tau  resolves the conceptual confusion of a "negative time." The observer's "now" is at  \tau=0 , and all observations probe into  \tau>0 , the past. The geometric origin of the time arrow is clear and unambiguous. 
>  · Equivalence of Observers: The model requires  R  to be a universal constant. All observers for whom  r_{\text{observer}} = R  are in a perfectly symmetric position, inherently satisfying the cosmological principle. 
>  · Static Spacetime: The "static" nature of the model refers to a background geometry that does not evolve with time. All observed cosmological phenomena are interpreted as consequences of this eternal geometry.

> 3.3 Dialogue with General Relativity and the Cosmic Censorship Hypothesis

> This framework proposes a novel perspective on the Cosmic Censorship Hypothesis. The naked singularity it describes is not located in the future but is embedded in the causal past of every observer. It acts as a past boundary condition for the observable universe, defining the beginning of causal history and the arrow of time. In this view, it does not lead to a breakdown of predictability but serves as the fundamental cause of all observable phenomena, offering a new path to address the conceptual issues surrounding the initial singularity in ΛCDM.

>  4.2 A Dynamical Vision: The Cosmic Engine

> While the present work establishes a static geometric framework, it naturally points toward a more profound, dynamical interpretation of the cosmic center. We speculate that the naked singularity may act as a "cosmic engine," fulfilling a dual role. In its capacity as a singularity, it is the future endpoint, gravitationally attracting and ultimately accreting light and matter. In its manifestation as a naked singularity, it becomes the past source, potentially recycling this accreted material via processes involving ultra-high-energy electromagnetic and particle radiation.

> This accretion-recycling mechanism could provide a physical origin for the cosmic microwave background and ultra-high-energy cosmic rays, reconceptualizing them not as relics of a hot Big Bang but as ongoing emissions from the active horizon. New matter, ejected from this cosmic engine, would lose kinetic energy on equipotential surfaces far from the center, condensing under gravity to form new galaxies. These galaxies would then begin their infall, completing a grand cosmic cycle within a steady-state topology.

> The fractal structure of the universe, with its characteristic sheet-like and filamentary features (e.g., the "God's Pizza" and "God's Finger" effects in redshift surveys), would then emerge naturally from the dynamics between different gravitational equipotential surfaces.

> This compelling vision sets the stage for our future work. The immediate next steps, however, are to solidify the geometric foundation presented here:

> 1. Deriving the underlying spacetime metric of this model from first principles. 
>  2. Conducting a detailed quantitative comparison of the geometric predictions (e.g., the  Z = \ln(R/r)  relation) with high-redshift data. 
>  3. Identifying definitive observational tests to distinguish this static, horizon-centric geometry from the expanding universe paradigm. The investigation of the proposed dynamical cycle, while central to the long-term development of the theory, hinges upon first establishing its geometric viability.
